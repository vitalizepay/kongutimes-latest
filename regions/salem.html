சேலம் மத்திய சிறைச்சாலையில் பாதுகாப்பு மற்றும் கண்காணிப்புப் பணிகள் தீவிரப்படுத்தப்பட்டுள்ள நிலையில், கோயம்புத்தூர் சிறையிலிருந்து பிரபல கைதி ஒருவர் அதிரடியாக சேலம் சிறைக்கு மாற்றப்பட்டுள்ளார். கோயம்புத்தூர் பகுதியைச் சேர்ந்த பிரபல ரவுடி ஒருவரைச் சிறைக்குள் வைத்தே கொலை செய்வதற்கு, மற்றொரு கைதி பெரும் தொகையை பேரம் பேசியதாக எழுந்த அதிர்ச்சித் தகவலைத் தொடர்ந்து இந்த நடவடிக்கை எடுக்கப்பட்டுள்ளது. சிறை வளாகத்திற்குள் கைதிகள் தங்களுக்குள் சதித்திட்டம் தீட்டிய விவகாரம் தமிழக சிறைத்துறை வட்டாரத்தில் பெரும் பரபரப்பை ஏற்படுத்திய நிலையில், உளவுத்துறையின் எச்சரிக்கையைத் தொடர்ந்து இந்த அதிரடி இடமாற்றம் சேலம் சிறைக்கு மேற்கொள்ளப்பட்டுள்ளது.

இந்த விவகாரத்தின் பின்னணி குறித்து விசாரித்தபோது, கோயம்புத்தூர் சிறையில் அடைக்கப்பட்டிருந்த கைதி ஒருவர், தனக்கு எதிரியாகக் கருதும் மற்றொரு ரவுடியை தீர்த்துக்கட்ட சிறையிலிருந்தவாறே திட்டமிட்டுள்ளார். இதற்காக சக கைதிகள் மற்றும் வெளியில் உள்ள தனது கூட்டாளிகளுடன் பல லட்ச ரூபாய் மதிப்பிலான பணப் பரிவர்த்தனை மற்றும் பேரம் பேசியதாக சிறைத்துறையினருக்கு ரகசியத் தகவல் கிடைத்தது. சிறைச்சாலை என்பது சீர்திருத்தப் பள்ளியாகச் செயல்பட வேண்டும் என்ற நோக்கில் இருக்கும்போது, அங்கேயே கொலைச் சம்பவத்திற்கு சதித்திட்டம் தீட்டப்பட்டிருப்பது சிறைத்துறையின் பாதுகாப்புக் குறைபாட்டை வெளிச்சமிட்டுக் காட்டியுள்ளது. இதனால், சம்மந்தப்பட்ட கைதியை உடனடியாக வேறு மாவட்டச் சிறைக்கு மாற்ற வேண்டிய கட்டாயம் அதிகாரிகளுக்கு ஏற்பட்டது.

இதன் காரணமாக, சேலம் மத்திய சிறைச்சாலையானது மிகவும் பாதுகாப்பான மற்றும் தீவிர கண்காணிப்பு வசதிகள் கொண்ட சிறை என்பதால், சதித்திட்டத்தில் ஈடுபட்ட அந்தக் கைதியை இங்கு மாற்ற அதிகாரிகள் முடிவு செய்தனர். பலத்த போலீஸ் பாதுகாப்புடன் கோயம்புத்தூரிலிருந்து கொண்டு வரப்பட்ட அந்தக் கைதி, சேலம் மத்திய சிறையில் உள்ள அதிமுக்கிய மற்றும் தனிமைப்படுத்தப்பட்ட உயர் பாதுகாப்புப் பிரிவில் அடைக்கப்பட்டுள்ளார். சேலம் சிறையில் ஏற்கனவே பல்வேறு மாவட்டங்களைச் சேர்ந்த முக்கியக் குற்றவாளிகள் அடைக்கப்பட்டுள்ள நிலையில், தற்போதைய புதிய கைதியின் வருகையால் சிறை வளாகத்தில் கூடுதல் காவலர்கள் பாதுகாப்புப் பணியில் ஈடுபடுத்தப்பட்டுள்ளனர்.

இந்தச் சம்பவம் சேலம் மாவட்ட மக்கள் மற்றும் சமூக ஆர்வலர்கள் மத்தியில் பெரும் அதிர்ச்சியையும் கவலையையும் ஏற்படுத்தியுள்ளது. சிறைக்குள் இருக்கும் கைதிகள் தங்களுக்குள் மோதிக் கொள்வதும், வெளியில் நடக்கும் குற்றச் சம்பவங்களுக்கு சிறையிலிருந்தே ஸ்கெட்ச் போடுவதும் பொதுமக்களின் பாதுகாப்பிற்கு அச்சுறுத்தலாக மாறும் என்று சமூக ஆர்வலர்கள் கவலை தெரிவிக்கின்றனர். குறிப்பாக, சேலம் மாநகரப் பகுதியில் சட்டம் ஒழுங்கு சீராக இருக்கும் வேளையில், பிற மாவட்டங்களின் குற்றப் பின்னணி கொண்ட கைதிகள் இங்கு மாற்றப்படும் போது, உள்ளூர் அளவிலும் அவர்களின் கூட்டாளிகளின் நடமாட்டம் அதிகரிக்க வாய்ப்புள்ளதாக மக்கள் அச்சமடைகின்றனர்.

இது குறித்து சேலம் சிறைத்துறை உயர் அதிகாரிகள் தரப்பில் கேட்டபோது, சிறைச்சாலையின் பாதுகாப்பு மற்றும் ஒழுங்குமுறைகளை நிலைநிறுத்துவதில் எவ்வித சமரசமும் செய்துகொள்ளப்பட மாட்டாது என்று உறுதியளித்தனர். சேலம் சிறைக்கு மாற்றப்பட்டுள்ள புதிய கைதி முழுமையான கண்காணிப்பு வளையத்திற்குள் கொண்டு வரப்பட்டுள்ளார் என்றும், அவர் மற்ற கைதிகளுடன் தொடர்பு கொள்ளாதவாறு 24 மணி நேரமும் சிசிடிவி கேமராக்கள் மூலம் கண்காணிக்கப்பட்டு வருகிறார் என்றும் கூறினர். மேலும், சிறை வளாகத்திற்குள் தடை செய்யப்பட்ட பொருட்கள் ஏதேனும் புழக்கத்தில் உள்ளதா என்பதை அறிய அவ்வப்போது திடீர் சோதனைகள் நடத்தப்படும் என்றும், கைதிகளின் நடவடிக்கைகள் தொடர்ந்து தீவிரமாகக் கண்காணிக்கப்படும் என்றும் அதிகாரிகள் திட்டவட்டமாகத் தெரிவித்துள்ளனர்.

In a significant move aimed at maintaining law and order within the state's correctional facilities, prison authorities have transferred a notorious inmate to the Salem Central Prison under tight security. The transfer was initiated following shocking revelations that the prisoner, while incarcerated in another facility, had been actively negotiating a financial deal to execute a contract killing of a rival gangster. Sensing an immediate threat to prison security and the potential for a violent gang war spilling onto the streets, senior officials of the Department of Prisons and Correctional Services sanctioned the emergency relocation. The Salem Central Prison, known for its high-security enclosures and stringent surveillance systems, was selected to isolate the conspirator from his active network.

The inmate at the center of this controversy has been identified as a history-sheeter with a long record of violent crimes, including extortion, assault, and attempted murder. Investigations revealed that he had been utilizing illicit communication channels from behind bars to negotiate a lucrative deal, running into lakhs of rupees, with external mercenaries to eliminate a prominent rival. Intelligence wings of the police department intercepted these communications, exposing the elaborate plot before it could be executed. By shifting him to the heavily fortified Salem facility, authorities aim to completely sever his contact with outside accomplices and neutralise his operational capabilities.

The news of a major murder conspiracy being orchestrated from within a prison has sent shockwaves through the local community, raising serious concerns among the residents of Salem and neighboring regions. Law-abiding citizens have expressed deep anxiety over the persistence of gang culture and the apparent ease with which incarcerated criminals manage to coordinate violent activities on the outside. Neighborhood welfare associations in Salem have called for more robust policing and proactive intelligence gathering to ensure that local youths are not drawn into these criminal networks as contract killers. Many residents feel that such high-profile transfers, while necessary, also bring an element of risk to the local area, necessitating heightened vigilance by the city police.

In response to these growing public apprehensions, the Salem district police administration and prison officials have assured the public of foolproof security measures. The Superintendent of the Salem Central Prison confirmed that the newly transferred inmate has been placed in a high-security solitary confinement block, away from the general prison population. Round-the-clock manual sentry duty has been deployed, and the existing closed-circuit television (CCTV) network is being utilized to monitor his movements constantly. Furthermore, regular surprise inspections and baggage scans are being conducted to prevent the smuggling of mobile phones, SIM cards, or any other contraband that could facilitate communication with the outside world.

This decisive action by the prison department underscores a broader crackdown on organized crime syndicates operating across Tamil Nadu. Senior police commanders in the Salem division have reiterated their zero-tolerance policy toward gang violence and jailhouse conspiracies. Local intelligence units have also been instructed to monitor the associates of the transferred prisoner who might be active in the Western belt. Through these coordinated efforts, the administration hopes to restore public confidence, maintain absolute discipline within the prison walls, and ensure that Salem remains safe from the spillover effects of inter-gang rivalries.

Local railway authorities have urged passengers to plan their journeys accordingly and cooperate with the department during this temporary disruption. The maintenance block is scheduled to take place during specific hours of the day, directly impacting commuters who rely on daily passenger and express trains connecting Salem with neighboring districts.

Among the affected services, the Coimbatore to KSR Bengaluru Uday Express (Train No. 22666) will be partially cancelled between Erode and Salem junctions. Similarly, the return service, the Bengaluru to Coimbatore Uday Express (Train No. 22665), will also face partial cancellation, operating only up to Salem and remaining suspended between Salem and Erode. This sudden suspension of services has caused significant concern among daily commuters, particularly office-goers, students, and business professionals who travel frequently between Salem, Erode, and Coimbatore. Railway officials have clarified that these measures are unavoidable as the structural integrity of the tracks must be upgraded periodically to prevent any untoward incidents.

The impact of this partial cancellation is being felt heavily across Salem district, where thousands of residents depend on the rail network for affordable and timely travel. Local merchant associations and regular commuters have expressed their grievances, noting that the mid-week disruption has forced many to seek alternative modes of transportation such as state-run buses and private cabs. Bus stands in Salem, including the Central Bus Stand, have witnessed a sudden surge in passenger footfall since early morning, leading to crowded boarding points and longer waiting times. Residents have requested the Tamil Nadu State Transport Corporation (TNSTC) to operate additional special buses between Salem and Erode to handle the extra passenger rush caused by the train cancellations.

In response to the public inconvenience, the Salem Railway Division administration has set up dedicated help desks and information counters at the Salem Junction railway station to assist stranded passengers. Station officials are actively guiding travelers regarding alternative train routes, refund procedures, and connecting bus services available nearby. Public address systems at the station are continuously broadcasting updates about the altered schedules to keep the public informed and minimize confusion on the platforms. Senior railway engineers monitoring the project have assured the public that the engineering work is progressing rapidly and is on track to be completed within the stipulated timeframe, allowing normal train operations to resume by late evening.

This temporary disruption highlights the critical role that the Salem railway network plays in the socio-economic fabric of the Kongu region. Local welfare associations have emphasized the need for better prior communication regarding such maintenance blocks so that the public can make alternative travel arrangements well in advance. While acknowledging the necessity of safety-related engineering works, community leaders in Salem have urged the railway ministry to schedule such maintenance during late-night hours or weekends in the future to minimize the impact on weekly working days. As the railway department works to restore full services, commuters in Salem remain hopeful that the upgraded infrastructure will lead to safer and faster journeys in the coming days.

In a decisive move to curb the rising tide of traffic violations and enhance road safety across the region, transport department officials in Salem district have temporarily suspended the driving licenses of 1,874 individuals over the past five months. This stringent disciplinary action, spanning from April to August of this year, targets motorists who have repeatedly flouted essential traffic regulations. According to senior officials from the regional transport offices (RTOs) in Salem, this massive crackdown is part of an ongoing, coordinated campaign involving both the local traffic police and transport authorities. The joint initiative aims to send a clear, uncompromising message to offenders that reckless driving will face severe legal consequences in the district.

The drastic measure of license suspension was not taken lightly, as officials identified specific, high-risk violations that directly contribute to fatal road accidents in Salem. A significant majority of the suspensions were handed down to individuals caught driving under the influence of alcohol, speeding past designated limits, and using mobile phones while operating vehicles. Additionally, overloading commercial vehicles and carrying passengers in goods carriages were cited as prominent reasons for the punitive action. Transport authorities emphasized that these specific offenses represent a blatant disregard for human life and are the primary catalysts for the high rate of road casualties recorded in the district's urban and rural sectors.

This aggressive enforcement drive has elicited a mixed yet largely positive response from the residents of Salem district. Many daily commuters and pedestrian advocacy groups have welcomed the strict measures, noting that the visible presence of checking inspectors has already begun to instill a sense of discipline among motorists on major arterial roads like the Salem-Bangalore Highway. However, some local residents have expressed concerns regarding the need for more comprehensive public awareness campaigns alongside punitive actions, arguing that many rural motorists remain unaware of the severe legal repercussions of minor traffic infractions. Nevertheless, the general consensus among the public is that such stringent enforcement is absolutely necessary to transform Salem’s roads into safer corridors for everyone.

In response to the public feedback and the ongoing challenges of traffic management, senior transport officials have defended the scale of the suspensions, stating that saving lives remains their topmost priority. Department spokespersons revealed that the regional transport offices in Salem East, Salem West, and Salem South have been instructed to conduct daily, randomized vehicular checks at key intersections and accident-prone zones. Furthermore, officials warned that repeat offenders would face permanent cancellation of their driving privileges, along with hefty monetary fines and potential legal prosecution. The department is also planning to integrate advanced surveillance technology, including speed guns and automatic number plate recognition cameras, to monitor traffic flow more effectively.

Looking ahead, the transport department, in collaboration with the Salem district administration, plans to complement these enforcement drives with educational seminars at local schools, colleges, and commercial transport hubs. By educating young drivers and professional heavy-vehicle operators about the critical importance of defensive driving and adherence to speed limits, authorities hope to foster a long-term culture of road safety. The ultimate goal of this dual approach of strict enforcement and continuous education is to drastically reduce the annual road accident mortality rate in Salem. For now, the suspension of nearly two thousand licenses in just five months stands as a stark reminder to all motorists in the district that safe driving is no longer optional, but a strictly enforced mandate.

இது குறித்து சேலம் மாவட்ட உயர் அதிகாரி ஒருவர் கூறுகையில், "மத்திய அரசின் வெளியுறவுத்துறை அமைச்சகம் மற்றும் வங்கதேசத் தூதரகத்தின் முழு ஒத்துழைப்போடு இந்த நாடு கடத்தும் நடவடிக்கை சட்டப்பூர்வமாகச் செய்து முடிக்கப்பட்டுள்ளது. முகாமில் இருந்தவர்களின் மனித உரிமைகளும், அடிப்படைத் தேவைகளும் அவர்கள் தங்கியிருந்த காலம் முழுவதும் முறையாகப் பேணப்பட்டன. இனிவரும் காலங்களிலும் சேலம் மாவட்டத்தில் சட்டவிரோதமாகத் தங்கும் வெளிநாட்டினரைக் கண்டறியும் சோதனைகள் தீவிரப்படுத்தப்படும்" என்று தெரிவித்தார். மேலும், முறையான ஆவணங்கள் இன்றி தங்குபவர்கள் மீது சட்டம் தன் கடமையைச் செய்யும் என்றும், அவர்களுக்குத் புகலிடம் தருபவர்கள் மீதும் கடுமையான நடவடிக்கை எடுக்கப்படும் என்றும் அவர் எச்சரித்துள்ளார்.

Fifty Bangladeshi nationals from Salem Attur camp deported to Kolkata under tight security

In a major administrative and diplomatic operation, fifty Bangladeshi nationals who were housed at the temporary holding camp in Attur, Salem district, have been successfully repatriated to their home country. These individuals had been detained at the high-security facility after being apprehended for violating various provisions of the Foreigners Act, including illegal entry and residing in the country without valid travel documents. Over the past few months, local authorities in Salem diligently coordinated with state intelligence departments, the Union Ministry of External Affairs, and the Bangladeshi High Commission to verify the identities of the detainees and facilitate their safe return. The completion of this repatriation process marks a significant step in resolving long-standing legal cases involving undocumented migrants in the western region of Tamil Nadu.

The repatriation process was executed under tight security, with a dedicated team of Salem district police personnel escorting the group from the Attur camp to the international border. Officials confirmed that the deportees were taken by train under heavy guard to the designated border outpost in West Bengal, where they were formally handed over to the Border Guard Bangladesh (BGB) authorities after final document verification. The deportees, who had spent several months in judicial custody and administrative detention in Salem, expressed relief at finally being able to return to their families. This coordinated effort required meticulous planning, as travel permits and emergency certificates had to be individually processed and approved by diplomatic channels before the physical transfer could take place.

The presence of the temporary camp in Attur had previously drawn significant attention from local residents and social activists, who raised questions regarding the living conditions and the legal status of the detainees. Local residents living in the vicinity of the camp expressed a sense of relief following the repatriation, noting that the heavy police presence and constant surveillance around the facility had created an atmosphere of unease in the neighborhood. Community leaders in Attur welcomed the government’s decisive action, stating that the speedy resolution of these cases prevents the prolonged detention of foreign nationals and eases the burden on local administrative resources. The departure of this large group has significantly reduced the occupancy of the Attur holding facility, allowing local authorities to streamline operations at the camp.

Senior administrative and police officials in Salem district highlighted that this repatriation reflects the government's commitment to upholding immigration laws while ensuring humanitarian standards are met during the deportation process. According to official statements, the district administration ensured that all basic amenities, medical check-ups, and necessary provisions were provided to the Bangladeshi nationals during their stay at the Attur camp. The Salem district police also emphasized that surveillance has been intensified across industrial pockets in the region to identify any further instances of illegal migration. Officials have urged local employers, particularly in the textile, agricultural, and manufacturing sectors of Salem, to strictly verify the identity and visa status of foreign laborers before hiring them.

This successful repatriation operation is expected to serve as a precedent for handling similar cases of undocumented foreign nationals currently detained in various parts of Tamil Nadu. Legal experts and human rights observers in the region have noted that timely diplomatic interventions are crucial to preventing indefinite detentions, which often burden the state treasury and complicate local law enforcement. With the Salem district administration successfully clearing this batch of repatriations, focus now shifts toward strengthening border monitoring and local intelligence networks to prevent illegal entry points. The local government’s swift execution of the repatriation directive has been widely appreciated as a balanced approach to national security and international humanitarian protocols.
