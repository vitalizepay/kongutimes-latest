சேலம் ஓமலூர் அருகே உள்ள காமலாபுரம் விமான நிலையத்தில் இருந்து சென்னைக்கு மீண்டும் தினசரி விமான சேவை தொடங்கப்பட்டுள்ளது பொதுமக்களிடையே பெரும் வரவேற்பைப் பெற்றுள்ளது. மத்திய அரசின் 'உடான்' திட்டத்தின் கீழ், அலையன்ஸ் ஏர் நிறுவனம் இந்த விமான சேவையை மீண்டும் இயக்க முன்வந்துள்ளது. கடந்த சில மாதங்களாக தொழில்நுட்ப காரணங்களாலும், போதிய பயணிகள் வருகை இல்லாததாலும் தற்காலிகமாக நிறுத்தப்பட்டிருந்த இந்த சேவை, தற்போது வணிகர்கள், தொழில் அதிபர்கள் மற்றும் பொதுமக்களின் தொடர் கோரிக்கைகளுக்கு இணங்க மீண்டும் பயன்பாட்டிற்கு வந்துள்ளது. இந்த சேவையின் மூலம் சேலம் மற்றும் அதன் சுற்றுவட்டார மாவட்ட மக்கள் சென்னைக்கு மிகக் குறைந்த நேரத்தில் பயணிக்க முடியும் என்பது குறிப்பிடத்தக்கது.

இந்த புதிய விமான சேவையின் மூலம் சேலம் மாவட்டத்தின் பொருளாதார வளர்ச்சி மற்றும் தொழில் துறை மேம்பாடு அடைவதற்கான வாய்ப்புகள் பெருமளவில் அதிகரித்துள்ளன. சேலம் மாவட்டத்தில் ஜவ்வரிசி உற்பத்தி, வெள்ளி ஆபரணத் தொழில், விசைத்தறி மற்றும் எஃகு ஆலைகள் போன்ற பல்வேறு முக்கிய தொழில்கள் மிகச் சிறப்பாக நடைபெற்று வருகின்றன. இங்குள்ள தொழிலதிபர்கள் தங்களது வணிக ரீதியான தேவைகளுக்காகவும், அவசர கூட்டங்களில் பங்கேற்பதற்காகவும் அடிக்கடி சென்னைக்குச் செல்ல வேண்டிய கட்டாயம் உள்ளது. சாலை மார்க்கமாகவும், ரயில் மூலமாகவும் சென்னைக்குச் செல்ல பல மணி நேரம் ஆகும் நிலையில், இந்த வான்வழிப் போக்குவரத்து அவர்களின் நேரத்தை மிச்சப்படுத்தி தொழிலை அடுத்த கட்டத்திற்கு கொண்டு செல்ல பெரிதும் உதவும் என எதிர்பார்க்கப்படுகிறது.

அலையன்ஸ் ஏர் நிறுவனத்தின் அதிகாரிகள் தெரிவித்துள்ள தகவலின்படி, இந்த விமான சேவையானது வாரத்தின் அனைத்து நாட்களிலும் இயக்கப்பட உள்ளது. சென்னையில் இருந்து காலை வேளையில் புறப்படும் இந்த விமானம், சேலம் காமலாபுரம் விமான நிலையத்தை வந்தடைந்து, பின்னர் இங்கிருந்து மீண்டும் சென்னைக்கு இயக்கப்படும் வகையில் கால அட்டவணை வடிவமைக்கப்பட்டுள்ளது. இதற்கான கட்டண முறைகளும் நடுத்தர மக்களும் பயன்படுத்தும் வகையில் ஏதுவாக நிர்ணயம் செய்யப்பட்டுள்ளதாக அதிகாரிகள் தரப்பில் தெரிவிக்கப்பட்டுள்ளது. மேலும், பயணிகளின் வருகை மற்றும் தேவையை உன்னிப்பாக கவனித்து, வரும் நாட்களில் கூடுதல் சேவைகளை வழங்குவது குறித்தும், மற்ற முக்கிய நகரங்களுக்கு சேவைகளை விரிவுபடுத்துவது குறித்தும் ஆலோசிக்கப்படும் என்று விமான நிலைய அதிகாரிகள் தரப்பில் கூறப்பட்டுள்ளது.

இந்த விமான சேவை மீண்டும் தொடங்கப்பட்டதற்கு சேலம் மாவட்ட மக்கள் மற்றும் பல்வேறு வர்த்தக அமைப்பினர் தங்களது மனமார்ந்த நன்றியினையும் மகிழ்ச்சியினையும் தெரிவித்துள்ளனர். குறிப்பாக, அவசர மருத்துவ சிகிச்சைக்காக சென்னைக்குச் செல்ல வேண்டிய நோயாளிகளுக்கு இந்த சேவை ஒரு வரப்பிரசாதமாக அமையும் என்று சமூக ஆர்வலர்கள் நெகிழ்ச்சியுடன் குறிப்பிடுகின்றனர். சேலம் மாவட்டத்தைச் சேர்ந்த ஐடி ஊழியர்கள் மற்றும் உயர்கல்வி பயிலும் மாணவர்கள் வார இறுதி நாட்களில் தங்களது சொந்த ஊருக்கு வந்து செல்ல இந்த சேவை மிகவும் பயனுள்ளதாக இருக்கும். அரசு நிர்வாகமும், மாவட்ட நிர்வாகமும் இணைந்து இந்த விமான நிலையத்தின் உள்கட்டமைப்பு வசதிகளை மேலும் மேம்படுத்த வேண்டும் என்பதே இப்பகுதி மக்களின் தற்போதைய பிரதான கோரிக்கையாக உள்ளது.

விமான போக்குவரத்து அமைச்சக அதிகாரிகள் மற்றும் சேலம் விமான நிலைய இயக்குனர் ஆகியோர் இந்த சேவை குறித்து பேசுகையில், காமலாபுரம் விமான நிலையத்தில் பயணிகளுக்கான பாதுகாப்பு மற்றும் இதர அடிப்படை வசதிகள் அனைத்தும் சர்வதேச தரத்தில் தற்பொழுது தயார் செய்யப்பட்டுள்ளதாக உறுதி அளித்துள்ளனர். பயணிகள் எவ்வித தடையுமின்றி பயணிக்க தேவையான அனைத்து பாதுகாப்பு சோதனைகளும், குடிநீர் மற்றும் இருக்கை வசதிகளும் விரிவுபடுத்தப்பட்டுள்ளன. இந்த விமான சேவையை இப்பகுதி மக்கள் முழுமையாகப் பயன்படுத்தி ஆதரவு அளித்தால் மட்டுமே, இச்சேவை எவ்வித தங்கு தடையுமின்றி தொடர்ந்து தொய்வின்றி செயல்பட முடியும் என்று அதிகாரிகள் பொதுமக்களுக்கு வேண்டுகோள் விடுத்துள்ளனர். வரும் நாட்களில் பயணிகளின் எண்ணிக்கை அதிகரிக்கும் பட்சத்தில், சேலம் விமான நிலையம் மிகப்பெரிய போக்குவரத்து மையமாக உருவெடுக்கும் என்பதில் ஐயமில்லை.

The long-anticipated air connectivity between Salem and Chennai has officially been restored, bringing immense relief to the residents and business communities of the Kongu region. The flight services, which had been temporarily suspended due to various operational challenges and low passenger turnout in the past, have now resumed under the Union Government’s regional connectivity scheme. This crucial air link was inaugurated amidst high expectations from local trade bodies, who have been relentlessly lobbying for the reinstatement of the service to foster economic growth. The resumption of this flight is expected to significantly reduce travel time between the steel city of Salem and the state capital, Chennai, which otherwise takes over six hours by road or rail.

The decision to operate the flight service comes as a strategic move to boost the industrial and commercial potential of Salem district, which is a major hub for textiles, steel, sago, and agriculture. Local entrepreneurs and corporate executives had frequently complained about the lack of rapid transport infrastructure, which hindered swift business transactions and deterred potential investors from visiting the region. By re-establishing this aerial route, the aviation authorities aim to provide a seamless travel experience for business professionals, medical tourists, and academics who frequently commute between the two cities. The flight schedule has been meticulously planned to cater to same-day travellers, allowing them to complete their work in Chennai and return to Salem by evening.

The resident welfare associations and the Salem District Chamber of Commerce have welcomed this development with great enthusiasm, noting that it will put Salem back on the aviation map of India. For several months, local activists and politicians had raised this issue at various governmental levels, emphasizing that a functional airport is vital for the overall socio-economic development of the district. Residents have expressed hope that the flight services will remain consistent this time, urging the operating airline to maintain affordable tariff structures to ensure high occupancy rates. The convenience of a one-hour flight is also expected to benefit emergency medical patients who require urgent specialized treatment at advanced healthcare facilities in Chennai.

In response to the resumption, airport authorities and local administration officials in Salem have assured that all necessary infrastructure upgrades and security protocols have been successfully put in place. The Salem Airport, located at Kamalapuram, has undergone rigorous safety audits and facility enhancements to accommodate the incoming passengers and ensure smooth operations. District officials have also stated that they are working on improving public transport connectivity and taxi services from the city centre to the airport terminal to facilitate hassle-free commuting for passengers. Furthermore, plans are reportedly underway to explore additional routes connecting Salem to other major southern cities like Bengaluru and Hyderabad in the near future, depending on the sustained success of the Chennai sector.

This renewed air connectivity is poised to act as a major catalyst for the tourism sector of the region, particularly benefiting the nearby hill station of Yercaud, which attracts thousands of visitors annually. Tour operators believe that hassle-free flight options from Chennai will encourage affluent domestic and international tourists to explore the scenic landscapes of Salem district. Additionally, the local handloom and powerloom sectors, which export unique textile products globally, will now have faster access to international cargo hubs via Chennai. As the first flights successfully take off and land at the Kamalapuram runway, there is a palpable sense of optimism that this aviation link will permanently accelerate Salem’s journey toward becoming a primary economic powerhouse in Tamil Nadu.

The demonstrations, which erupted in areas like Tharamangalam and Mecheri, saw a large number of villagers, particularly women, blocking key arterial roads with empty plastic pots. The protestors alleged that they have not received a regular supply of potable drinking water for several weeks, forcing them to rely on expensive private water tankers or walk long distances to fetch water from agricultural wells. The sudden road blockades caused brief traffic disruptions in the rural sectors before local police and block development officials rushed to the spots to pacify the agitated crowds.

The primary trigger for the widespread public anger is the highly irregular supply schedule managed by the local panchayats, which has reportedly deteriorated over the last two months. Villagers pointed out that despite multiple petitions submitted to the block development offices and during the weekly grievance redressal meetings at the Salem Collectorate, no concrete action had been taken to resolve the supply bottlenecks. The residents expressed deep anguish that their daily lives and livelihoods have been severely disrupted, as they are forced to spend a significant portion of their day merely securing enough water for cooking and drinking.

The impact of this acute water scarcity has fallen disproportionately on women and daily wage labourers in the region, who are bearing the brunt of the administrative failure. Many women remarked that they are unable to go for their daily agricultural work on time because they have to wait for hours near public taps in the hope of receiving a few pots of water. Furthermore, the financial burden on these rural households has escalated significantly, as they are now forced to shell out hard-earned money to purchase water from private suppliers who have capitalized on the crisis by inflating their prices. The lack of clean drinking water has also raised serious health concerns among the villagers, who fear that prolonged reliance on untreated groundwater from agricultural borewells could lead to waterborne diseases, especially among young children and the elderly.

In response to the intensifying protests and road blockades, senior revenue and police officials, along with representatives from the Tamil Nadu Water Supply and Drainage (TWAD) Board, visited the protest sites to negotiate with the villagers. The officials attributed the irregular supply to ongoing maintenance work in the main distribution pipelines of the Mettur surplus water scheme and a temporary drop in the water levels of local sources due to the dry weather. They assured the protestors that emergency measures would be put in place immediately to address the shortfall, including the deployment of municipal water tankers to the worst-affected habitations on a rotational basis. The authorities also promised to conduct a thorough inspection of the distribution valves and rectify any pipeline leakages that might be hindering the equitable distribution of water.

Following written assurances from the block development officers that regular water supply would be restored within the next three days, the villagers finally agreed to withdraw their road blockades and disperse. However, the local community groups have warned the administration that they will launch an even larger, indefinite agitation if the promises are not fulfilled within the stipulated timeframe. Activists in Salem have urged the district administration to formulate a long-term, sustainable water management plan for the rural blocks, emphasizing that ad-hoc measures like tanker supply are only temporary band-aids to a systemic problem. As the summer months approach, the residents of Salem's rural belt remain highly vigilant, hoping that the government will take proactive steps to prevent another major water crisis in the district.

In a decisive move to curb the rising tide of traffic violations and enhance road safety across the region, transport department officials in Salem district have temporarily suspended the driving licenses of 1,874 individuals over the past five months. This stringent disciplinary action, spanning from April to August of this year, targets motorists who have repeatedly flouted essential traffic regulations. According to senior officials from the regional transport offices (RTOs) in Salem, this massive crackdown is part of an ongoing, coordinated campaign involving both the local traffic police and transport authorities. The joint initiative aims to send a clear, uncompromising message to offenders that reckless driving will face severe legal consequences in the district.

The drastic measure of license suspension was not taken lightly, as officials identified specific, high-risk violations that directly contribute to fatal road accidents in Salem. A significant majority of the suspensions were handed down to individuals caught driving under the influence of alcohol, speeding past designated limits, and using mobile phones while operating vehicles. Additionally, overloading commercial vehicles and carrying passengers in goods carriages were cited as prominent reasons for the punitive action. Transport authorities emphasized that these specific offenses represent a blatant disregard for human life and are the primary catalysts for the high rate of road casualties recorded in the district's urban and rural sectors.

This aggressive enforcement drive has elicited a mixed yet largely positive response from the residents of Salem district. Many daily commuters and pedestrian advocacy groups have welcomed the strict measures, noting that the visible presence of checking inspectors has already begun to instill a sense of discipline among motorists on major arterial roads like the Salem-Bangalore Highway. However, some local residents have expressed concerns regarding the need for more comprehensive public awareness campaigns alongside punitive actions, arguing that many rural motorists remain unaware of the severe legal repercussions of minor traffic infractions. Nevertheless, the general consensus among the public is that such stringent enforcement is absolutely necessary to transform Salem’s roads into safer corridors for everyone.

The department is also planning to integrate advanced surveillance technology, including speed guns and automatic number plate recognition cameras, to monitor traffic flow more effectively.

Looking ahead, the transport department, in collaboration with the Salem district administration, plans to complement these enforcement drives with educational seminars at local schools, colleges, and commercial transport hubs. By educating young drivers and professional heavy-vehicle operators about the critical importance of defensive driving and adherence to speed limits, authorities hope to foster a long-term culture of road safety. The ultimate goal of this dual approach of strict enforcement and continuous education is to drastically reduce the annual road accident mortality rate in Salem. For now, the suspension of nearly two thousand licenses in just five months stands as a stark reminder to all motorists in the district that safe driving is no longer optional, but a strictly enforced mandate.

The National Highway stretch running through Salem district has rapidly transformed into a perilous death trap, causing widespread panic and outrage among local residents. In the span of just a single month, this vital transit corridor has witnessed over thirty serious road accidents, resulting in numerous gruesome fatalities and leaving several others permanently maimed. What was once designed to be a high-speed arterial route facilitating seamless commerce and travel has now degenerated into what locals describe as a living hell. The sudden and alarming spike in vehicular collisions has shattered the peace of the surrounding neighborhoods, turning daily commutes into high-risk gambles for thousands of motorists and pedestrians who rely on this infrastructure.

A comprehensive analysis of the crisis reveals a deadly combination of structural engineering flaws, inadequate lighting, and a complete lack of speed-regulation mechanisms along the most vulnerable stretches of the highway. Local commuters and transport unions point out that poorly planned median crossings, blind curves, and the absence of prominent warning signages have actively contributed to the rising casualty count. During the night, the danger escalates exponentially as heavy multi-axle trucks and speeding luxury buses navigate the poorly lit Salem sections of the highway, frequently ramming into smaller passenger vehicles and two-wheelers. The absence of dedicated pedestrian underpasses has further exacerbated the situation, forcing villagers from adjacent settlements to risk their lives crossing the high-speed lanes on foot.

The human cost of this infrastructure failure has been devastating for the families residing along the Salem highway corridor. Local traders, school children, and daily wage laborers are forced to navigate these hazardous junctions every day under a constant shadow of fear. Community activists have highlighted that almost every household in the immediate vicinity has either lost a member to these road tragedies or knows someone who has suffered life-altering injuries. The economic impact on these families, who often lose their primary breadwinners to these sudden highway fatalities, has triggered a wave of silent desperation across the rural and semi-urban pockets of the district.

In response to the mounting public anger and the grim statistics, local administrative officials and the Salem district police have scrambled to initiate damage-control measures. Law enforcement authorities have promised to increase highway patrolling, install temporary barricades to curb speeding at identified accident hot spots, and deploy additional radar speed-guns to penalize reckless drivers. Furthermore, highway department officials have conducted preliminary inspections to identify immediate engineering interventions, such as installing high-mast lights and painting clearer road markings. However, residents remain highly skeptical of these reactive measures, dismissing them as temporary band-aids on a systemic problem that requires a complete structural overhaul of the highway design.

As the death toll continues to rise, community leaders and local representatives are demanding a permanent, high-level joint committee comprising National Highways Authority of India (NHAI) engineers and Salem district administrators to address the crisis. They assert that until permanent flyovers, pedestrian subways, and scientifically designed service roads are constructed at the major conflict points, the highway will continue to claim innocent lives. With public patience wearing thin, local action groups have warned of massive road blockades and protests if the government fails to implement concrete, long-term safety solutions immediately. For now, the people of Salem are left to navigate this treacherous stretch with utmost caution, praying that they do not become the next statistic on this increasingly lethal highway.
