சேலம் மாவட்ட ஆட்சியர் அலுவலகத்தில் உள்ள கூட்ட அரங்கில் இன்று மாவட்ட விவசாயிகள் கருத்து கேட்புக் கூட்டம் மிக முக்கியத்துவம் வாய்ந்த சூழலில் நடைபெற்றது. தமிழக சட்டப்பேரவையில் விரைவில் பட்ஜெட் கூட்டத்தொடர் நடைபெற உள்ள நிலையில், வேளாண்மை துறைக்கு என தனியாக தாக்கல் செய்யப்படவிருக்கும் பட்ஜெட்டிற்கான ஆலோசனைகள் மற்றும் கருத்துக்களைப் பெறுவதற்காக இந்த சிறப்புக் கூட்டம் ஏற்பாடு செய்யப்பட்டிருந்தது. இக்கூட்டத்தில் பல்வேறு மாவட்டங்களைச் சேர்ந்த விவசாயப் பிரதிநிதிகளும், தருமபுரி சட்டமன்ற உறுப்பினர் சௌமியா அன்புமணி உள்ளிட்ட மக்கள் பிரதிநிதிகளும் கலந்து கொண்டு தங்களது கோரிக்கைகளையும், விவசாயிகளின் எதிர்பார்ப்புகளையும் விரிவாக எடுத்துரைத்தனர். குறிப்பாக, சேலம் மற்றும் அதன் சுற்றுவட்டார மாவட்டங்களின் பாசனத் தேவைகள் மற்றும் விவசாயிகளின் வாழ்வாதாரப் பிரச்சினைகள் குறித்து இக்கூட்டத்தில் மிக விரிவாக விவாதிக்கப்பட்டது.

இக்கூட்டத்தில் பங்கேற்ற தருமபுரி சட்டமன்ற உறுப்பினர் சௌமியா அன்புமணி, பின்னர் செய்தியாளர்களைச் சந்தித்து விவசாயிகளின் முக்கிய கோரிக்கைகளை ஊடகங்கள் வாயிலாக அரசுக்கு வலியுறுத்தினார். அப்போது பேசிய அவர், சிறு மற்றும் குறு விவசாயிகளின் கூட்டுறவு கடன்களை முழுமையாக தள்ளுபடி செய்ய வேண்டும் என்பதே தற்போதைய முதன்மையான கோரிக்கையாக இருக்கிறது என்று அழுத்தமாகக் குறிப்பிட்டார். தற்போதைய நிலையில் சுமார் 6,000 கோடி ரூபாய் அளவிலான கூட்டுறவு கடன்கள் மட்டுமே தள்ளுபடி செய்யப்பட்டுள்ளதாகக் குறிப்பிட்ட அவர், இதனை முழுமையாகத் தள்ளுபடி செய்திருந்தால் மட்டுமே அது விவசாயிகளுக்கு உண்மையான பயனைத் தந்திருக்கும் என்று தெரிவித்தார். விவசாயிகள் தங்களின் வாழ்வாதாரத்தை மீட்டெடுக்கப் போராடி வரும் வேளையில், வரவிருக்கும் வேளாண் பட்ஜெட்டில் இதற்கான முழுமையான அறிவிப்பு வெளியாகும் என்று தங்களைப்போன்ற மக்கள் பிரதிநிதிகளும் விவசாயிகளும் பெரிதும் எதிர்பார்ப்பதாக அவர் தெரிவித்தார்.

மேலும், பாசனத் திட்டங்களை விரைந்து செயல்படுத்த வேண்டியதன் அவசியத்தையும், நதிநீர் இணைப்புத் திட்டங்களை காலவரையறைக்குள் முடிக்க வேண்டியதன் முக்கியத்துவத்தையும் சௌமியா அன்புமணி இக்கூட்டத்தில் வலியுறுத்தினார். கடந்த ஆண்டை ஒப்பிடுகையில் இந்த ஆண்டு விவசாய சாகுபடிப் பரப்பு கணிசமாகக் குறைந்து, தற்போது வெறும் 20 லட்சம் ஏக்கரில் மட்டுமே விவசாயம் நடைபெற்று வருவது கவலையளிப்பதாக அவர் சுட்டிக்காட்டினார். விவசாயத் தொழில் நலிவடைந்து வருவதைத் தடுத்து, அதிக வேலைவாய்ப்புகளை உருவாக்கும் துறையாக இதனை மாற்ற வேண்டும் என்றும், இன்றைய இளைஞர்கள் மதுப் பழக்கங்கள் போன்ற தவறான வழிகளில் இருந்து விடுபட்டு, மீண்டும் விவசாயத்தை நோக்கித் திரும்ப வேண்டும் என்றும் அவர் கேட்டுக்கொண்டார். இதற்கு ஏதுவாக அரசு விவசாயத்தை ஒரு லாபகரமான தொழிலாக மாற்றுவதற்கான பல்வேறு சிறப்புத் திட்டங்களையும், மானியங்களையும் பட்ஜெட்டில் அறிவிக்க வேண்டும் என்று அவர் வலியுறுத்தினார்.

இந்தக் கருத்து கேட்புக் கூட்டத்தின் ஏற்பாடுகள் குறித்து அதிருப்தி வெளியிட்ட சட்டமன்ற உறுப்பினர், இதற்கு முன்பு இரண்டு அல்லது மூன்று மாவட்ட விவசாயிகளை மட்டுமே அழைத்து இத்தகைய ஆலோசனைக் கூட்டங்கள் நடத்தப்பட்டதாகக் குறிப்பிட்டார். ஆனால், தற்போது ஒரே நேரத்தில் பத்து மாவட்டங்களைச் சேர்ந்த விவசாயிகளை ஒரே இடத்தில் கூட்டியதால், தங்களின் கருத்துக்களை முழுமையாகவும் விரிவாகவும் பதிவு செய்ய பல விவசாயிகளுக்குப் போதிய நேரமும் வாய்ப்பும் கிடைக்கவில்லை என்ற குறைபாட்டைச் சுட்டிக்காட்டினார். தமிழக முதலமைச்சர் விவசாயிகளைச் நேரில் சந்திக்க மறுப்பதாக எழுப்பப்படும் விமர்சனங்களுக்கு பதிலளித்த அவர், விவசாயிகளின் அனைத்து நியாயமான கோரிக்கைகளையும், அவர்களின் குமுறல்களையும் தாங்கள் சட்டமன்றத்தில் தொடர்ந்து ஓங்கி ஒலித்து வருவதாகவும், விவசாயிகளின் உரிமைகளுக்காக எப்போதும் பாடுபடுவோம் என்றும் உறுதியளித்தார்.

Dharmapuri MLA Soumya Anbumani demands full waiver of cooperative loans in Salem farmers meet

In a significant administrative gathering held at the Salem District Collectorate’s meeting hall, local farmers and policymakers congregated to voice their pressing grievances ahead of the upcoming Tamil Nadu legislative budget session. The consultative meeting, aimed at gathering grassroots feedback for the exclusive agricultural budget, saw active participation from several regional lawmakers, including Dharmapuri Member of Legislative Assembly (MLA) Sowmya Anbumani. Addressing the media following the session, the legislator emphasized the urgent need for comprehensive policy interventions to revive the agrarian economy in the region. The event highlighted the growing challenges faced by local cultivators and served as a critical platform for Salem's agricultural community to demand immediate state intervention.

A primary focus of the discussion was the demand for a complete waiver of cooperative agricultural loans, which local representatives argue is vital for the survival of small and marginal farmers. While acknowledging that the state government has recently waived loans worth six thousand crore rupees, Sowmya Anbumani asserted that a total, unconditional waiver of all outstanding cooperative debts would have provided much-needed relief to struggling families. The agrarian crisis in Salem and neighboring districts has intensified due to a notable decline in cultivable land, with active farming shrinking down to just twenty lakh acres this season. Local farmers have been staging various protests, and there is a strong expectation that the upcoming budget will address these financial burdens through decisive policy announcements.

The impact of these agricultural challenges is felt deeply by the rural residents of Salem, where farming remains the primary source of livelihood and employment. Consequently, several daily commuters and long-distance travelers relying on these specific services will need to adjust their travel plans for the day.

The partial cancellation is expected to primarily impact the local passengers, office-goers, and students who commute daily between Salem and its neighboring districts. While the department has expressed regret for the temporary inconvenience caused to the public, they emphasized that such periodic maintenance is non-negotiable for maintaining high safety standards. Temporary help desks and announcements are being set up at the Salem Junction to guide stranded passengers and provide real-time updates regarding alternative transit options.

Local residents and regular commuters in Salem have expressed mixed reactions to this sudden development, noting that while safety upgrades are welcome, mid-week disruptions cause significant logistical challenges. Many travelers who depend on budget-friendly rail transport are now forced to rely on state government buses, which are expected to experience a heavy rush throughout the day. Representatives from local commuter welfare associations have urged the railway administration to expedite the engineering works so that normal services can resume without further delay. As the day progresses, railway officials will continue to monitor the progress of the engineering works closely from the divisional headquarters in Salem. Regular announcements are being broadcasted across the station's public address system to keep the arriving passengers informed about the partial cancellations and minor delays of passing express trains. The administration has also requested commuters to cooperate with the railway staff during this maintenance window and to check the official helpline numbers or the National Train Enquiry System (NTES) app before heading to the station. Normal train operations through the Salem sector are expected to be fully restored by tomorrow morning once the track fitness certificate is officially issued.

The Dravidar Kazhagam (DK) held its high-profile general body meeting at a private marriage hall located near the bustling Five Roads junction in Salem, drawing significant attention from local residents and political observers across the district. Presided over by the veteran DK President K. Veeramani, the crucial gathering served as a platform to pass several landmark resolutions aimed at reforming the electoral process and addressing deep-seated social issues. Local residents in Salem closely monitored the event, as the decisions made here are expected to influence the regional political landscape and the socio-economic welfare of the Kongu region. The meeting highlighted the organization's strategic positioning in Tamil Nadu politics and its ongoing ideological campaigns.

Among the key resolutions passed during the Salem conclave, the Dravidar Kazhagam strongly urged the Union and State governments to amend the anti-defection law to prevent opportunistic political shifts. The organization proposed that any elected representative who resigns from their post to defect to another party for money, power, or personal gain should be legally barred from contesting any elections, including local body polls, for a period of five years. This resolution directly addresses growing concerns among Salem residents regarding political instability and the wastage of public taxpayers' money on frequent by-elections. By demanding a stricter constitutional framework, the DK aims to restore public faith in the democratic process and ensure that elected leaders remain accountable to their constituencies.

Addressing the media immediately after the general body meeting in Salem, DK chief K. Veeramani compared the current political climate in the state to a precarious hung assembly scenario, attributing this instability to the influence of northern states' political culture where defections are commonplace. He pointed out that historical precedents of defection have paved the way for modern political opportunism, where individuals with criminal backgrounds are often accommodated into political folds simply to satisfy their lust for power. While emphasizing that the relationship between the Dravidar Kazhagam and the ruling Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (DMK) remains as strong as a mother-child bond, Veeramani asserted that the DK would continue to offer principled support or constructive criticism to any ruling or opposition party based strictly on ideological alignment.

In addition to electoral reforms, the general body meeting passed crucial resolutions demanding the immediate implementation of a comprehensive caste-based census and the swift enactment of a dedicated law to prevent honor killings. Salem district, which has historically witnessed complex caste dynamics, stands to be significantly impacted by these proposed measures. Local social activists and residents have welcomed the call for a stringent anti-honor killing law, viewing it as a necessary step to protect young couples and foster social harmony in the region. The demand for a caste census was also framed as an essential requirement for ensuring equitable distribution of resources and educational opportunities among all communities in Salem and across the state.

While local administration officials in Salem declined to comment directly on the political resolutions, district authorities noted that security and logistical arrangements were adequately maintained around the Five Roads venue to prevent any public disruption during the high-profile event. The resolutions passed by the Dravidar Kazhagam have sparked intense discussions among Salem’s academic, social, and political circles, with many praising the focus on systemic electoral reforms. As the district prepares for future local body governance, the call to ban defectors from contesting municipal and panchayat elections has resonated deeply with the local electorate, who seek stable and honest governance free from political horse-trading.

The Salem district has witnessed a significant moment of solidarity for local sportspersons as native Indian cricketer Thangarasu Natarajan stepped forward to support a realistic narrative focusing on the struggles of athletes. The narrative of this project deeply resonates with the local sports climate of Salem, as it directly addresses the systemic corruption, administrative politics, and neglect that athletes endure. According to the creators, the plot revolves around a dedicated protagonist who takes legal action in court against administrative irregularities in sports bodies to secure justice for overlooked talents. By depicting the struggles of athletes who are denied opportunities due to favoritism and political interference, the project aims to serve as an eye-opener for both the public and policy makers. The creators emphasized that their objective is not merely to criticize the existing system, but to constructively bring the plight of underprivileged sportspersons into the mainstream consciousness of Tamil Nadu.

Local residents and young sports aspirants across Salem district have expressed immense pride and hope following Natarajan’s public endorsement of this cause. For many youngsters training in Salem's local academies, Natarajan is a living legend who overcame extreme poverty and lack of infrastructure to represent India on the global stage. Residents believe that having a sports icon of his stature highlight these administrative hurdles will force local athletic associations to be more transparent and supportive of grassroots talent. Parents of aspiring athletes in the district have also welcomed the initiative, stating that it realistically portrays the financial and emotional toll families undergo when their children are unfairly sidelined by sports administrators.

This development has sparked a wider conversation in Salem regarding the creation of better sports infrastructure, such as multi-purpose training facilities and financial stipends for underprivileged players. Local sports activists in the district are planning to leverage this momentum to petition the district administration for improved maintenance of local playfields and transparent selection trials. As Natarajan's endorsement continues to trend across social media platforms, the people of Salem remain hopeful that this collective push will dismantle the barriers preventing rural youth from achieving their sporting dreams. With the spotlight now firmly on the challenges of sports governance, Salem is positioning itself at the forefront of the advocacy for athletes' rights in the state.

In response to the anticipated surge in passenger traffic over the upcoming weekend, the Tamil Nadu State Transport Corporation (TNSTC) Salem Division has officially announced the operation of 200 special buses. This proactive measure is designed to accommodate the heavy rush of commuters traveling from and through the Salem district to various key destinations across the state. Transport officials noted that weekends consistently witness a significant rise in travelers, including working professionals, students, and families returning to their native places, which necessitates additional services beyond the standard daily schedule to prevent overcrowding at major terminuses.

These special buses will operate from major transport hubs within the Salem district, connecting passengers to prominent cities such as Chennai, Bengaluru, Madurai, Trichy, and Coimbatore. The deployment planning has been meticulously structured to ensure that high-demand routes receive a higher frequency of services, thereby minimizing waiting times for the public. By strategically positioning these vehicles at key boarding points, the TNSTC Salem Division aims to streamline the weekend exodus and provide a hassle-free travel experience for thousands of commuters who rely on public transportation.

The initiative has been warmly welcomed by local residents and regular commuters of the Salem region, who frequently face difficulties securing reserved seats during weekend rushes. Many travelers expressed relief, stating that the introduction of these 200 special buses would curb the menace of private operators charging exorbitant fares during peak travel windows. Residents also highlighted that the systematic operation of government buses ensures safety, reliability, and affordability, making it easier for families to plan their weekend journeys without the anxiety of last-minute cancellations or inflated ticket prices.

Senior officials from the TNSTC Salem Division have assured the public that all necessary arrangements, including the deployment of additional technical staff and crew members, have been finalized to ensure the smooth running of these services. Special monitoring teams will be stationed at the Salem Central Bus Stand and other key transit points to oversee passenger flow, manage queues, and dispatch buses based on real-time demand. The administration has also emphasized that all vehicles undergo rigorous maintenance checks prior to deployment to guarantee passenger safety and minimize the chances of mid-journey breakdowns.

To further facilitate convenient travel, transport department authorities have urged passengers to utilize the online ticket booking facilities offered by the state corporation. Booking seats in advance is highly recommended as it allows the department to assess passenger volume more accurately and make further operational adjustments if required. Through this comprehensive and well-coordinated effort, the Salem division of the transport corporation continues to demonstrate its commitment to public service, ensuring that the district's transport infrastructure robustly supports the regional mobility needs of its citizens.
