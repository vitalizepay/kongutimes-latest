தர்மபுரி மாவட்டத்தில் கடந்த சில மாதங்களாகப் போதிய மழையின்மை மற்றும் கடுமையான கோடை வெயில் காரணமாக ஒகேனக்கல் காவிரி ஆற்றில் நீர்வரத்து மிகக் கடுமையாகக் குறைந்துள்ளது. தர்மபுரி மாவட்ட மக்களின் முக்கிய குடிநீர் ஆதாரமாக விளங்கும் ஒகேனக்கல் கூட்டுக்குடிநீர் திட்டத்தின் நீர் உந்து நிலையங்களில் நீர்மட்டம் கணிசமாகச் சரிந்துள்ளது. இதன் காரணமாக, தர்மபுரி நகராட்சிப் பகுதிகள் மற்றும் மாவட்டத்தின் பல்வேறு கிராமப்புறப் பகுதிகளுக்கு வழங்கப்படும் குடிநீர் விநியோகம் பெரும் பாதிப்புக்குள்ளாகியுள்ளது. வழக்கமாகத் தடையின்றி வழங்கப்பட்டு வந்த குடிநீர், தற்போது பல நாட்களுக்கு ஒருமுறை மட்டுமே விநியோகிக்கப்படுவதால் பொதுமக்கள் அன்றாடத் தேவைகளுக்குப் பெரும் சிரமத்தை எதிர்கொண்டு வருகின்றனர்.

கர்நாடக காவிரி நீர் பிடிப்புப் பகுதிகளில் போதிய மழை இல்லாததாலும், அம்மாநில அணைகளில் இருந்து தமிழகத்திற்குத் திறந்துவிடப்படும் நீரின் அளவு குறைக்கப்பட்டதாலும் ஒகேனக்கல் நீர்வீழ்ச்சிக்கு வரும் நீரின் அளவு வரலாறு காணாத வகையில் சரிந்துள்ளது. இதனால் ஒகேனக்கல் குடிநீர் திட்டத்தின் கீழ் செயல்படும் நீரேற்று நிலையங்களில் உள்ள கிணறுகளில் நீர்மட்டம் மிகவும் கீழே இறங்கிவிட்டது. இந்த நீர் பற்றாக்குறை காரணமாக, மாவட்ட நிர்வாகத்தால் திட்டமிட்டபடி முழு கொள்ளளவு தண்ணீரைச் சுத்திகரித்து விநியோகம் செய்ய இயலவில்லை. தர்மபுரி மாவட்டத்தின் புவியியல் அமைப்பு மற்றும் நிலத்தடி நீர்மட்டம் ஏற்கனவே குறைவாக உள்ள நிலையில், இந்த ஆற்று நீர் குறைவு ஒட்டுமொத்த மாவட்டத்தின் குடிநீர் கட்டமைப்பையே அசைத்துப் பார்த்துள்ளது.

இந்தக் கடுமையான குடிநீர் தட்டுப்பாட்டினால் தர்மபுரி, பென்னாகரம், பாலக்கோடு, காரிமங்கலம் மற்றும் பாப்பிரெட்டிப்பட்டி உள்ளிட்ட பல்வேறு வட்டாரங்களைச் சேர்ந்த லட்சக்கணக்கான பொதுமக்கள் கடுமையாகப் பாதிக்கப்பட்டுள்ளனர். வாரத்திற்கு ஒருமுறை அல்லது பத்து நாட்களுக்கு ஒருமுறை மட்டுமே குடிநீர் விநியோகம் செய்யப்படுவதால், பொதுமக்கள் தண்ணீரை விலைக்கு வாங்கும் அவல நிலைக்குத் தள்ளப்பட்டுள்ளனர். ஏழை மற்றும் நடுத்தர மக்கள் தங்களின் அன்றாட வருமானத்தில் பெரும் பகுதியை லாரிகள் மற்றும் கேன்களில் விற்கப்படும் குடிநீருக்காகச் செலவிட வேண்டியுள்ளது. மேலும், விவசாயப் பணிகளுக்கும் போதிய தண்ணீர் கிடைக்காமல் பயிர்கள் கருகும் நிலைக்குத் தள்ளப்பட்டுள்ளதால், கிராமப்புற மக்களின் வாழ்வாதாரம் முற்றிலுமாக முடங்கியுள்ளது.

இது குறித்து தர்மபுரி மாவட்ட நிர்வாகம் மற்றும் தமிழ்நாடு குடிநீர் வடிகால் வாரிய உயர் அதிகாரிகள் தீவிர ஆலோசனை மேற்கொண்டு வருகின்றனர். தற்போதைய நீர் பற்றாக்குறையைச் சமாளிக்க மாற்று ஏற்பாடுகள் செய்யப்பட்டு வருவதாகவும், ஒகேனக்கல் ஆற்றுப் படுகையில் உள்ள ஆழ்துளைக் கிணறுகளைத் தூர்வாரி கூடுதல் நீரைப் பெற நடவடிக்கை எடுக்கப்பட்டு வருவதாகவும் அதிகாரிகள் தெரிவித்துள்ளனர். மேலும், பொதுமக்களுக்குச் சுழற்சி முறையில் லாரிகள் மூலம் குடிநீர் வழங்க ஏற்பாடுகள் செய்யப்பட்டு வருவதாகவும், நீர் விநியோகத்தை முறைப்படுத்தத் தனிக்குழுக்கள் அமைக்கப்பட்டுள்ளதாகவும் அவர்கள் குறிப்பிட்டுள்ளனர். பொதுமக்கள் தண்ணீரை வீணாக்காமல் சிக்கனமாகப் பயன்படுத்த வேண்டும் என்றும் அதிகாரிகள் தரப்பில் வேண்டுகோள் விடுக்கப்பட்டுள்ளது.

Residents across several parts of Dharmapuri district are facing severe hardships due to an acute shortage and irregular supply of drinking water, triggered by a significant drop in the water levels at the Hogenakkal reservoir. The Hogenakkal water supply scheme, which serves as the primary source of potable water for the entire district, has been severely impacted by the dwindling inflows into the Cauvery River. As the dry season intensifies, the water levels at the intake points have plummeted, making it increasingly difficult for municipal authorities to pump and distribute adequate water to the households. Many residential areas that used to receive daily water supply are now forced to wait for several days, leading to widespread public frustration and anxiety.

The crisis has severely disrupted the daily routines of local citizens, particularly homemakers and daily wage labourers who must now spend hours securing water for their basic needs. In many suburban and rural pockets of Dharmapuri, residents have been forced to rely on private water tankers, which have significantly hiked their prices due to the surging demand. Poor and middle-class families are worst hit, as they cannot afford to purchase private water regularly and are forced to queue up at public borewells and community taps, often late into the night. Local community activists report that the water scarcity has also begun to affect local businesses and small-scale commercial establishments, which depend heavily on a steady municipal supply to run their daily operations.

In response to the growing public outcry, senior officials from the Tamil Nadu Water Supply and Drainage (TWAD) Board and local administrative bodies have initiated emergency review meetings to address the distribution bottlenecks. Officials have acknowledged the technical challenges in pumping water from the depleted Hogenakkal reservoir and have blamed the lack of seasonal rainfall in the catchment areas for the current crisis. To manage the available resources, the administration has introduced a temporary rationing system, altering the distribution schedule to ensure that at least a minimal quantum of water reaches every neighborhood on a rotational basis. Engineering teams are also inspecting the pipelines and pumping machinery to identify and rectify any leakages that could worsen the water loss during transit.

The district administration has appealed to the public to cooperate with the authorities during this challenging period and to use the distributed water with utmost care and economy. Officials have strictly warned against the illegal use of suction pumps to draw water directly from the main distribution lines, promising severe action and confiscation of equipment against violators. Furthermore, the local body representatives have been instructed to identify dry pockets and deploy government-contracted water tankers to distribute free drinking water to the most vulnerable communities on a priority basis. While these temporary measures have provided minor relief to some areas, residents continue to demand a more sustainable, long-term solution to the district's recurring water woes.

As the summer heat intensifies, the pressure on the Dharmapuri district administration to resolve the crisis continues to mount. Local civic groups are urging the state government to allocate special funds for desilting the Hogenakkal reservoir and upgrading the existing distribution infrastructure to prevent such shortages in the future. Experts suggest that rainwater harvesting and the rejuvenation of traditional water bodies across the district must be prioritized to reduce the heavy reliance on the Cauvery River. Until substantial inflows resume at Hogenakkal or a major spell of rain brings relief, the people of Dharmapuri will have to brace themselves for a challenging season of strict water conservation and reliance on temporary administrative interventions.

The residents of Dharmapuri district are currently grappling with a severe drinking water crisis as the water levels at the Hogenakkal reservoir have plummeted significantly. The reservoir, which serves as the primary source for the Hogenakkal Drinking Water Sub-Mission Project, has seen its inflows dwindle due to the lack of adequate rainfall in the catchment areas. Consequently, the municipal administration and rural local bodies have been forced to slash the volume of water distributed daily. This sudden drop in supply has disrupted the daily routines of thousands of households across the district, leaving citizens desperate for alternative solutions.

The impact of this acute water shortage is being felt severely across both urban and rural pockets of Dharmapuri. Residents have complained that the tap water supply, which used to be provided either daily or once every two days, has now become highly irregular, with some areas receiving water only once a week or even once every ten days. To meet their basic survival needs, families are being forced to purchase expensive private tanker water or trek long distances to fetch water from agricultural borewells. Women and the elderly are bearing the brunt of this crisis, spending several hours of their day queuing up at public handpumps that are also slowly running dry.

Local activists and community leaders have pointed out that the current crisis is a result of both natural factors and inadequate preparatory planning by the water board authorities. While the dry season has naturally reduced the flow of the Cauvery River into Hogenakkal, critics argue that the maintenance of the distribution pipelines and local overhead tanks has been neglected. Silt accumulation at the intake points has further reduced the efficiency of the pumping systems, compounding the misery of the local population. The public is demanding immediate intervention from the district administration to regulate private water suppliers who are allegedly exploiting the situation by charging exorbitant rates.

In response to the growing public outcry, senior officials from the Tamil Nadu Water Supply and Drainage (TWAD) Board and the Dharmapuri district administration have inspected the Hogenakkal intake sites to assess the ground reality. Officials have assured the public that emergency measures are being put in place to mitigate the crisis, including the deployment of government-contracted water tankers to the worst-affected neighborhoods. Plans are also underway to temporarily draw water from alternative local sources and borewells to supplement the Hogenakkal supply until the seasonal rains begin. The administration has appealed to the residents to use the available drinking water sparingly and avoid using it for non-essential purposes like gardening or washing vehicles.

As the summer heat intensifies, the pressure on the local government to deliver a sustainable solution continues to mount. Community groups in Dharmapuri have warned of staging widespread road blockades and protests if the water supply is not regularized within the coming days. The district collector has directed local block development officers and municipal commissioners to monitor the distribution process personally to ensure equitable water sharing. For now, the people of Dharmapuri remain on high alert, praying for early monsoon showers to replenish the parched Hogenakkal reservoir and restore normalcy to their lives.

Looking ahead, the district administration plans to complement these law enforcement measures with awareness campaigns targeting vulnerable demographics across Dharmapuri. Educational institutions will host interactive sessions to educate students about the severe legal and health hazards associated with drug consumption. Meanwhile, the police department is preparing to fast-track the legal proceedings against the 23 arrested individuals to send a strong deterrent message to other offenders. Through this dual approach of strict policing and community-level sensitization, Dharmapuri aims to foster a safer, drug-free environment for its future generations.

The historic district of Dharmapuri in northwestern Tamil Nadu is currently grappling with a severe drinking water crisis as water levels at the crucial Hogenakkal reservoir have plummeted significantly. The reservoir, which serves as the primary source for the Hogenakkal Water Supply Scheme, has seen its inflows dwindle to alarming lows due to a prolonged dry spell and deficient rainfall in the catchment areas of the Cauvery basin. This hydrological deficit has severely crippled the regional water distribution network, leaving municipal authorities struggling to maintain routine supply schedules. Consequently, tens of thousands of households across the district are now facing unprecedented disruptions in their daily lives, highlighting the region's acute vulnerability to seasonal climatic shifts.

The direct fallout of this environmental crisis is being acutely felt by the local residents, who are now subjected to highly irregular and unpredictable municipal water supply cycles. In several residential pockets of Dharmapuri town and adjacent rural areas, tap water is being supplied only once every ten to fifteen days, forcing citizens to rely heavily on expensive private water tankers. Domestic chores have come to a virtual standstill, and the lack of potable water has raised serious public health concerns among the local population. Small business owners, particularly those in the hospitality and service sectors, are also reporting severe operational difficulties as their daily overhead costs have spiked due to the emergency purchase of private water.

In response to the mounting public outcry, senior officials from the Tamil Nadu Water Supply and Drainage (TWAD) Board and local municipal authorities have initiated emergency mitigation measures. Engineers are working around the clock to optimize the extraction process at the intake wells in Hogenakkal and are exploring alternative groundwater sources to supplement the current deficit. Administrative teams have also deployed temporary water tankers to the worst-affected residential zones to ensure that basic drinking water needs are met on a rotational basis. District administrators have urged the public to practice strict water conservation methods and avoid using potable water for non-essential activities like gardening and vehicle washing during this critical period.

Local community activists and resident welfare associations have expressed deep dissatisfaction with the administration's reactive approach, pointing out that seasonal fluctuations in the Cauvery River are predictable. Critics argue that the local government failed to implement adequate desiltation projects and rainwater harvesting infrastructure during the monsoon season, which could have cushioned the district against this inevitable dry spell. Public demonstrations have erupted in several parts of the district, with angry citizens demanding a long-term, sustainable solution to Dharmapuri’s chronic water woes rather than temporary stop-gap measures. The protestors have warned of intensified agitations if the district administration fails to restore regular supply within the coming week.

As the summer heat intensifies across the Kongu region, the immediate outlook for Dharmapuri remains highly dependent on seasonal rain spells in neighboring Karnataka's catchment zones. Until substantial inflows resume into the Cauvery River to replenish the Hogenakkal reservoir, the district administration faces the monumental task of equitably rationing the available water resources. For now, the residents of Dharmapuri must endure a difficult period of rationing and high expenditure, serving as a stark reminder of the urgent need for comprehensive water management reforms in Tamil Nadu's drought-prone interior districts.
