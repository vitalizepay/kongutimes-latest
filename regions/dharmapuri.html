தமிழகத் தொழில்நுட்பக் கல்வி இயக்ககம் வெளியிட்ட நடப்பு கல்வி ஆண்டிற்கான பொறியியல் கலந்தாய்வு தரவரிசைப் பட்டியலில், தர்மபுரி மாவட்டத்தைச் சேர்ந்த அரசுப் பள்ளி மாணவி மாநில அளவில் முதலிடம் பிடித்து சாதனை படைத்துள்ளார். தர்மபுரி மாவட்டம், பாலக்கோடு அடுத்த மாரண்டஹள்ளி அரசு பெண்கள் மேல்நிலைப் பள்ளியில் படித்து, பிளஸ்-2 பொதுத்தேர்வில் 600-க்கு 580 மதிப்பெண்கள் பெற்ற மாணவி பிரியா, பொறியியல் தரவரிசையில் அரசுப் பள்ளி மாணவர்களுக்கான 7.5 சதவீத உள் ஒதுக்கீட்டுப் பிரிவில் 200-க்கு 200 கட்-ஆப் மதிப்பெண்கள் பெற்று இந்த அரிய சாதனையை நிகழ்த்தியுள்ளார். எளிய விவசாயக் குடும்பப் பின்னணியைக் கொண்ட இந்த மாணவியின் சாதனை, ஒட்டுமொத்த தர்மபுரி மாவட்டத்திற்கும், தமிழக அரசுப் பள்ளி மாணவர்களுக்கும் பெரும் பெருமிதத்தைத் தேடித்தந்துள்ளது.

வறுமையான சூழலிலும், முறையான வழிகாட்டுதலோடு விடாமுயற்சியுடன் படித்ததே இந்த வரலாற்றுச் சாதனையை எட்ட முக்கியக் காரணமாக அமைந்துள்ளது. மாணவியின் தந்தை ஒரு சாதாரண விவசாயக் கூலித் தொழிலாளி என்பதால், குடும்பத்தின் பொருளாதார நெருக்கடிகளுக்கு மத்தியிலும் கல்வி ஒன்றே தங்களின் வாழ்வை உயர்த்தும் என்ற உயரிய நோக்கில் மாணவி பிரியா தனது படிப்பில் முழுக் கவனத்தையும் செலுத்தியுள்ளார். பள்ளியில் ஆசிரியர்கள் நடத்திய பாடங்களை அன்றாடம் கூர்ந்து கவனித்து, சந்தேகங்களைத் உடனுக்குடன் தீர்த்துக்கொண்டதும், முறையான திருப்புதல் தேர்வுகளை எழுதியதுமே பொறியியல் கட்-ஆப் மதிப்பெண்ணில் முழு மதிப்பெண்கள் பெற தனக்கு வழிவகுத்ததாக மாணவி நெகிழ்ச்சியுடன் தெரிவித்துள்ளார்.

இந்த வரலாற்றுச் சாதனை தர்மபுரி மாவட்டத்தின் கிராமப்புறப் பகுதிகளில் வாழும் ஏழை, எளிய மாணவர்களிடையே மிகப்பெரிய நேர்மறைத் தாக்கத்தையும், புதிய நம்பிக்கையையும் ஏற்படுத்தியுள்ளது. அரசுப் பள்ளிகளில் படிக்கும் மாணவர்களாலும் மாநில அளவில் முதலிடம் பிடித்து சாதிக்க முடியும் என்பதை மாரண்டஹள்ளி மாணவி பிரியா நிரூபித்துக் காட்டியுள்ளார். இந்தத் தகவல் மாவட்டத்தின் பல்வேறு அரசுப் பள்ளிகளில் பயிலும் மாணவ, மாணவிகளுக்கு உயர்கல்வி மீதான ஆர்வத்தை அதிகரித்துள்ளதுடன், தர்மபுரி மாவட்டத்தைச் சேர்ந்த பல சமூக ஆர்வலர்களும், பொதுமக்களும் மாணவியின் வீட்டிற்கு நேரில் சென்று தங்களது வாழ்த்துகளையும், பாராட்டுகளையும் தெரிவித்து வருகின்றனர்.

மாணவியின் இந்த அசாத்திய சாதனை குறித்து தர்மபுரி மாவட்ட முதன்மைக் கல்வி அலுவலர் மற்றும் பள்ளித் தலைமை ஆசிரியர் ஆகியோர் தங்களது நெஞ்சம் நிறைந்த வாழ்த்துகளையும் அதிகாரப்பூர்வ கருத்துகளையும் பகிர்ந்துள்ளனர். அரசுப் பள்ளி மாணவர்களின் கல்வித் தரத்தை உயர்த்த தமிழக அரசு எடுத்து வரும் பல்வேறு சிறப்பு முயற்சிகளும், ஆசிரியர்களின் அர்ப்பணிப்புடன் கூடிய கற்பித்தல் பணியுமே இத்தகைய மாநில அளவிலான சாதனைகளுக்கு முக்கிய அடித்தளம் என்று அவர்கள் பெருமிதத்துடன் குறிப்பிட்டனர். மேலும், மாணவி பிரியா விரும்பும் உயர்தரப் பொறியியல் கல்லூரியில் கணினி அறிவியல் பிரிவில் சேர்ந்து படிப்பதற்கான அனைத்து வழிகாட்டுதல்களையும், தேவையான உதவிகளையும் கல்வித்துறை முழுமையாக வழங்கும் என்றும் அவர்கள் உறுதியளித்துள்ளனர்.

In a stellar achievement that has brought immense pride to the entire Kongu region, a female student from the rural hinterlands of Dharmapuri district has secured the coveted first rank at the state level in the newly released Tamil Nadu Engineering Admissions (TNEA) merit list. The outstanding performance of this young scholar has highlighted the academic potential brewing in the western districts of the state, proving that determination can overcome geographical and socio-economic barriers. Local residents and educational activists across Dharmapuri have erupted in celebration, hailing her success as a monumental milestone for girls' education in the region. The achievement is being viewed not just as an individual victory, but as a collective triumph for a district that has historically strived to improve its literacy rates and higher education enrollment numbers.

The stellar academic feat was achieved through rigorous preparation and unwavering focus, culminating in a perfect cut-off score that placed her at the absolute top of the highly competitive engineering merit list. Coming from a modest background in Dharmapuri, the student creditably managed to outshine lakhs of aspirants from metropolitan cities like Chennai and Coimbatore, who often have access to superior coaching infrastructure and resources. Family members and school teachers expressed their boundless joy, noting that her disciplined study routine and deep conceptual understanding of mathematics and science paved the way for this historic milestone. This remarkable success story has quickly become a beacon of inspiration for thousands of government and private school students across the district who aspire to pursue professional courses in premier institutions.

The impact of this achievement on the local community in Dharmapuri has been profoundly positive, fostering a renewed sense of confidence among parents regarding the potential of local educational institutions. Neighbors, community leaders, and fellow students have been visiting the achiever’s residence to offer their congratulations and present traditional shawls as a mark of respect. Many local residents remarked that this success would break the stereotype that quality technical education and top ranks are reserved only for urban elites. Educationalists in the region believe this will encourage more conservative families in the rural blocks of Dharmapuri to support their daughters in pursuing higher professional education rather than settling for early marriages or general streams.

In response to this extraordinary accomplishment, district administration officials and senior bureaucrats from the school education department visited the student to formally felicitate her on behalf of the state. The District Collector of Dharmapuri praised her exemplary dedication and remarked that the administration is committed to upgrading school infrastructure to nurture more such talents from the region. Official sources confirmed that special counseling and guidance would be provided to help her secure a seat in her desired branch at the prestigious College of Engineering, Guindy (CEG) or other top-tier institutions. Furthermore, the education department has announced plans to document her study methods to motivate other government school students under the state’s welfare schemes.

Looking ahead, this state-level breakthrough is expected to catalyze a positive shift in the academic landscape of Dharmapuri, prompting both government and private schools to enhance their training methodologies for professional entrance processes. Local social welfare organizations have already appealed to the government to establish more specialized coaching centers in the district to support rural students aiming for national and state-level academic honors. As the young topper prepares to embark on her engineering journey, her success remains a shining testament to the fact that talent, when coupled with hard work, knows no boundaries. The people of Dharmapuri continue to celebrate this historic moment, hopeful that this is just the beginning of a golden era of academic excellence for the district.

As the summer heat intensifies across the Kongu region, the immediate outlook for Dharmapuri remains highly dependent on seasonal rain spells in neighboring Karnataka's catchment zones. Until substantial inflows resume into the Cauvery River to replenish the Hogenakkal reservoir, the district administration faces the monumental task of equitably rationing the available water resources. The sudden drop in reservoir levels has caught many local administrative bodies off guard, leading to major disruptions in the daily lives of the local population.

The impact of this water scarcity is being felt acutely across several residential colonies and villages throughout Dharmapuri, where tap water supply has become highly irregular. This prolonged gap has forced residents, particularly those from lower-income backgrounds, to rely heavily on private water tankers, which have subsequently hiked their prices to cash in on the demand. Local women, who bear the primary burden of fetching water, are forced to walk long distances to public borewells or agricultural wells to secure even a few pots of drinking water for their families.

As the hot weather continues to intensify across the region, the district administration is under immense pressure to restore regular supply before the situation escalates into a full-blown public protest. The landmark Hogenakkal Drinking Water Fluorosis Mitigation Project, designed to provide safe and clean drinking water to the chronically parched regions of Dharmapuri district, is currently failing to meet the basic needs of local residents due to highly erratic supply. Across several residential areas in the district, citizens are expressing deep anguish over the unpredictable distribution schedules, which have left them without adequate water for days on end. What was once hailed as a permanent solution to the region's severe groundwater fluorosis crisis has now become a source of daily anxiety for thousands of households. Local communities are forced to wait indefinitely for the municipal taps to run, disrupting their daily routines and throwing domestic life into complete disarray.

The root cause of the current crisis lies in a combination of deteriorating distribution infrastructure, frequent pipeline leakages, and operational inefficiencies within the local administrative machinery. Residents point out that the main transmission lines from the Hogenakkal intake point frequently suffer from pressure drops and technical snags, which are rarely addressed with any sense of urgency. Furthermore, the lack of a standardized and publicly communicated supply timetable has exacerbated the situation, leaving households unable to plan their water storage effectively. Despite the abundance of water at the source point of the Cauvery River, the local distribution network in Dharmapuri remains plagued by maintenance bottlenecks that prevent the water from reaching the end consumers consistently.

The impact on the daily lives of the residents in Dharmapuri has been profoundly disruptive, forcing many to find expensive alternatives to meet their basic survival needs. Families are increasingly forced to rely on private water tankers, which have capitalized on the artificial scarcity by charging exorbitant rates that middle and lower-income households can ill afford. Women, who traditionally bear the burden of managing household water requirements, are spending sleepless nights waiting for the taps to trickle, often to be disappointed by low-pressure supply that barely fills a few pots. This ongoing water insecurity has not only strained family budgets but has also raised fears of a resurgence in the use of local groundwater, which is notoriously contaminated with high levels of fluoride.

In response to the growing public outcry and mounting protests from civic groups, local administrative officials and representatives from the Tamil Nadu Water Supply and Drainage (TWAD) Board have acknowledged the distribution bottlenecks. Officials have stated that the irregular supply is primarily due to ongoing maintenance work, power fluctuations at the pumping stations, and the need to replace aging pipelines in specific sectors of the district. They have assured the public that dedicated technical teams are working to rectify the pressure discrepancies and seal existing leaks along the main distribution lines. Additionally, local authorities have promised to establish a more transparent and reliable supply schedule to ensure that all neighborhoods receive their fair share of water.

As the summer months approach, the pressure on the local administration to resolve this crisis permanently is mounting rapidly. Residents and social activists in Dharmapuri are demanding not just temporary fixes, but a comprehensive audit of the entire Hogenakkal water distribution network to identify systemic vulnerabilities. They argue that without a robust maintenance framework and strict accountability for municipal staff, the multi-crore project will fail to deliver on its primary promise of safeguarding public health. Until these structural reforms are implemented and consistent water supply is restored, the people of Dharmapuri remain trapped in a frustrating cycle of scarcity and administrative apathy.

Residents across several parts of Dharmapuri district are facing severe hardships due to the highly erratic supply of drinking water under the landmark Hogenakkal Drinking Water Ramification Scheme. Despite the project being specifically designed to alleviate the fluorosis crisis and water scarcity in the region, households in major residential areas are reportedly receiving water only once every ten to fifteen days. The prolonged gaps between supply cycles have forced families to rely on expensive private water tankers or unsafe local groundwater sources, defeating the very purpose of the multi-crore government initiative.

The crisis has deepened over the summer months as municipal overhead tanks fail to fill up regularly, leading to a breakdown in the scheduled distribution of potable water. Local community activists and residents point out that while the source at Hogenakkal has sufficient flow, technical glitches in the pipeline network, frequent power outages at pumping stations, and alleged mismanagement by local administrative bodies have choked the supply chain. In several suburban pockets of Dharmapuri, the pressure in the distribution lines is so low that even when water is released, it fails to reach the tail-end households, leaving hundreds of families stranded without a drop of clean drinking water.

The impact of this erratic supply is felt most acutely by daily wage labourers and lower-income families who cannot afford to buy commercial canned water. Women in these neighborhoods are forced to walk long distances in the early hours of the morning to fetch water from agricultural borewells, which are often high in fluoride content and pose significant health risks over the long term. Many residents have expressed deep frustration, noting that they are still being billed regularly for water connections by the local bodies despite receiving virtually no service for weeks on end.

In response to the growing public outcry and spontaneous street protests in some parts of the district, senior officials from the Tamil Nadu Water Supply and Drainage (TWAD) Board and local municipal authorities have acknowledged the distribution bottlenecks. Officials have attributed the current disruption to ongoing maintenance work, pipeline leaks caused by road widening projects, and occasional voltage fluctuations that disrupt the heavy pumping machinery at the main headworks. They have assured the public that dedicated engineering teams are working round-the-clock to rectify the distribution anomalies and restore regular supply to all affected wards.

To prevent further escalation of the crisis, local administrative officers have promised to deploy temporary water tankers to the worst-affected neighborhoods in Dharmapuri until the pipeline pressures are fully stabilized. Meanwhile, resident welfare associations have demanded a transparent, published timetable for water distribution so that households can plan their storage accordingly. They have also urged the state government to conduct a comprehensive audit of the Hogenakkal water scheme's local infrastructure to permanently resolve these recurring supply failures before the onset of the next dry season.

இந்த நீர் பற்றாக்குறையினால் தர்மபுரி நகராட்சிப் பகுதிகள், பென்னாகரம், பாலக்கோடு மற்றும் காரிமங்கலம் ஆகிய ஒன்றியங்களுக்கு உட்பட்ட நூற்றுக்கணக்கான கிராம மக்கள் கடுமையாகப் பாதிக்கப்பட்டுள்ளனர். வாரத்திற்கு இருமுறை முறைவழியாக வழங்கப்பட்டு வந்த குடிநீர், தற்போது பத்து நாட்களுக்கு ஒருமுறை அல்லது அதற்கும் மேலாகவே விநியோகிக்கப்படும் அவலநிலை உருவாகியுள்ளது. இதனால் அன்றாடத் தேவைகளுக்கே தண்ணீரை விலைக்கு வாங்க வேண்டிய நிலைக்கு ஏழை மற்றும் நடுத்தரக் குடும்பங்கள் தள்ளப்பட்டுள்ளதாக அப்பகுதி சமூக ஆர்வலர்கள் வேதனை தெரிவிக்கின்றனர்.

பொதுமக்களின் இந்தத் துயரத்தைக் களைவது குறித்து மாவட்ட நிர்வாகம் மற்றும் குடிநீர் வடிகால் வாரிய அதிகாரிகள் தரப்பில் ஆலோசனைகள் மேற்கொள்ளப்பட்டு வருகின்றன. தற்போதைய சூழலைக் கட்டுப்படுத்த மாற்று ஏற்பாடுகளாக ஆழ்துளைக் கிணறுகளைப் பயன்படுத்துவது குறித்தும், லாரிகள் மூலம் தற்காலிகமாகத் தண்ணீர் விநியோகம் செய்வது குறித்தும் திட்டமிட்டு வருவதாக அதிகாரிகள் தெரிவித்துள்ளனர். மேலும், நீர் இழப்பைத் தடுக்கவும், சட்டவிரோதக் குடிநீர் இணைப்புகளைக் கண்டறிந்து துண்டிக்கவும் சிறப்புப் படைகள் அமைக்கப்பட்டு கண்காணிப்புப் பணிகள் தீவிரப்படுத்தப்பட்டுள்ளதாக அவர்கள் குறிப்பிட்டுள்ளனர்.

இருப்பினும், தர்மபுரி மாவட்டத்தின் குடிநீர்ப் பிரச்னைக்கு நிரந்தரத் தீர்வு காண போர்க்கால அடிப்படையில் நடவடிக்கைகள் எடுக்கப்பட வேண்டும் என்று உள்ளூர் மக்கள் கோரிக்கை விடுத்துள்ளனர். வறட்சிக் காலங்களில் ஒகேனக்கல் திட்டத்தைச் சீராக இயக்கத் தேவையான கூடுதல் உள்கட்டமைப்பு வசதிகளை மேம்படுத்துவது அவசியமாகிறது. மாவட்ட நிர்வாகம் உரிய நேரத்தில் தலையிட்டு, குடிநீர் விநியோகத்தை முறைப்படுத்தி, அனைத்துப் பகுதி மக்களுக்கும் தடையின்றித் தண்ணீர் கிடைப்பதை உறுதி செய்ய வேண்டும் என்பதே தற்போதைய ஒட்டுமொத்த தர்மபுரி மக்களின் எதிர்பார்ப்பாக உள்ளது.

Dharmapuri faces severe water crisis as Hogenakkal reservoir levels dip rapidly

The residents of Dharmapuri district are currently grappling with a severe drinking water crisis as the water levels in the vital Hogenakkal reservoir have plummeted significantly. This drastic dip in the reservoir's capacity has severely disrupted the local water distribution network, leaving tens of thousands of households with highly irregular and inadequate supply. Local municipal bodies and panchayats, which rely heavily on this source for their daily distribution schedules, are now struggling to maintain even basic supply lines to the affected residential areas. The sudden scarcity has forced many families to ration their daily water usage, disrupting domestic routines and raising widespread concerns across the region.

The primary cause behind this escalating crisis is the prolonged dry spell and the lack of sufficient inflow into the Cauvery River basin, which feeds the Hogenakkal water supply scheme. Due to weak seasonal rainfall in the catchment areas and reduced releases from upstream dams, the storage levels at the Hogenakkal intake points have reached critically low margins. Engineering teams managing the distribution infrastructure have noted that the low pressure caused by the depleted reservoir levels makes it technically challenging to pump water to elevated areas and distant villages across the Dharmapuri district. Consequently, the intervals between water supply cycles have stretched from the usual 24 to 48 hours to once every four to six days in several severely affected neighborhoods.

The impact of this water shortage is being felt acutely by local residents, particularly homemakers, daily wage laborers, and small business owners who cannot afford private water tankers. Many citizens are now forced to wait for hours at public communal taps or travel long distances to fetch water from agricultural borewells. The situation has also led to a burgeoning private water tanker market, where operators are reportedly capitalizing on the crisis by charging exorbitant rates for substandard water. Local community activists have expressed deep frustration over the lack of prior warning from the administration, pointing out that poorer households are bearing the brunt of this logistical failure.

In response to the growing public outcry, senior officials from the Tamil Nadu Water Supply and Drainage (TWAD) Board and local administrative bodies have conducted emergency inspection meets to assess the situation. Authorities have assured the public that they are actively working on contingency plans, including the deployment of government-operated mobile water tankers to the worst-hit pockets of the district. Engineers are also exploring the feasibility of tapping alternative local sources, such as deep borewells and community open wells, to temporarily supplement the main supply line until inflows to the reservoir improve.

To prevent the situation from deteriorating further, district administrators have issued a strict advisory urging residents to practice water conservation and avoid using potable water for non-essential purposes like gardening or washing vehicles. Local body officials have been instructed to closely monitor the distribution process to ensure an equitable supply of water to all wards, preventing any hoarding or illegal tapping of pipelines. While the administration remains hopeful that upcoming atmospheric depressions will bring much-needed rainfall to recharge the reservoir, residents of Dharmapuri continue to demand a more robust, long-term solution to safeguard their basic water security against seasonal fluctuations.
