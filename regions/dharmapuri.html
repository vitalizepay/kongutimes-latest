A shocking incident of public violence sent waves of panic through the Dharmapuri B-Traction bus stand on Friday, when a young woman was brutally attacked by her estranged lover in broad daylight. The victim, identified as a young resident of the district, had her throat slit by the assailant following a heated altercation in the crowded transit hub. Commuters and local vendors, who initially stood frozen in disbelief, quickly intervened as the attacker attempted to flee the scene. The swift and aggressive intervention of the public prevented the assailant from escaping, but it also resulted in a severe vigilante beating before law enforcement could arrive.

According to preliminary investigations conducted by the local police, the horrific attack was motivated by a personal dispute and unrequited love, as the suspect had reportedly been stalking and harassing the victim over her decision to end their relationship. On the day of the incident, the suspect confronted the victim at the bus stand, demanding that she reconcile with him. When she steadfastly refused his advances and attempted to walk away, the enraged youth drew a sharp weapon and slashed her neck in front of dozens of onlookers. The sudden outbreak of violence left the bustling bus terminal in a state of utter chaos, with passengers running for safety before a brave group of bystanders managed to overpower the armed assailant.

Following the brutal assault, both the victim and the severely beaten assailant were rushed to the Dharmapuri Government Medical College Hospital for emergency treatment. Medical authorities have confirmed that the young woman, who was initially admitted in critical condition due to severe blood loss, has miraculously regained consciousness after undergoing emergency surgery. Meanwhile, the attacker is also receiving medical attention under tight police security at the same facility, where he is being treated for the multiple injuries he sustained during the public thrashing. Hospital sources indicate that while the victim remains under close observation in the intensive care unit, her vital signs have stabilized, bringing immense relief to her family and well-wishers.

The gruesome incident has deeply unsettled the residents of Dharmapuri, raising serious concerns regarding the safety of women in public spaces and the adequacy of security patrols at major transit hubs. Local merchants and regular commuters expressed their outrage over the audacity of the criminal, noting that the B-Traction bus stand is typically one of the most heavily crowded areas in the town. Many residents are now demanding the permanent deployment of visible police personnel and the installation of high-definition CCTV cameras across the entire terminal to deter such heinous acts of violence in the future. Activists in the district have also called for stricter surveillance and swifter judicial action against stalkers to prevent similar tragedies.

In response to the public outcry, senior officials of the Dharmapuri district police have assured the public that a comprehensive investigation is underway and that stringent legal action will be taken against the perpetrator. A formal case has been registered under various sections of the Indian Penal Code, including attempt to murder, and a dedicated police team has been stationed at the hospital to monitor the suspect until he is officially fit for remand. The police administration has also promised to increase patrolling in and around all major bus stands and educational institutions in the district. Authorities have urged the public to immediately report any instances of harassment or stalking to the police helpline, promising swift intervention to ensure public safety.

Water inflow to Hogenakkal Falls in Dharmapuri decreases to 8,000 cusecs

The residents of Dharmapuri district are currently grappling with a severe drinking water crisis as the water levels at the Hogenakkal reservoir have plummeted significantly. The reservoir, which serves as the primary source for the Hogenakkal Drinking Water Sub-Mission Project, has seen its inflows dwindle due to the lack of adequate rainfall in the catchment areas. Consequently, the municipal administration and rural local bodies have been forced to slash the volume of water distributed daily. This sudden drop in supply has disrupted the daily routines of thousands of households across the district, leaving citizens desperate for alternative solutions.

The impact of this acute water shortage is being felt severely across both urban and rural pockets of Dharmapuri. Residents have complained that the tap water supply, which used to be provided either daily or once every two days, has now become highly irregular, with some areas receiving water only once a week or even once every ten days. To meet their basic survival needs, families are being forced to purchase expensive private tanker water or trek long distances to fetch water from agricultural borewells. Women and the elderly are bearing the brunt of this crisis, spending several hours of their day queuing up at public handpumps that are also slowly running dry.

Local activists and community leaders have pointed out that the current crisis is a result of both natural factors and inadequate preparatory planning by the water board authorities. While the dry season has naturally reduced the flow of the Cauvery River into Hogenakkal, critics argue that the maintenance of the distribution pipelines and local overhead tanks has been neglected. Silt accumulation at the intake points has further reduced the efficiency of the pumping systems, compounding the misery of the local population. The public is demanding immediate intervention from the district administration to regulate private water suppliers who are allegedly exploiting the situation by charging exorbitant rates.

In response to the growing public outcry, senior officials from the Tamil Nadu Water Supply and Drainage (TWAD) Board and the Dharmapuri district administration have inspected the Hogenakkal intake sites to assess the ground reality. Officials have assured the public that emergency measures are being put in place to mitigate the crisis, including the deployment of government-contracted water tankers to the worst-affected neighborhoods. Plans are also underway to temporarily draw water from alternative local sources and borewells to supplement the Hogenakkal supply until the seasonal rains begin. The administration has appealed to the residents to use the available drinking water sparingly and avoid using it for non-essential purposes like gardening or washing vehicles.

As the summer heat intensifies, the pressure on the local government to deliver a sustainable solution continues to mount. Community groups in Dharmapuri have warned of staging widespread road blockades and protests if the water supply is not regularized within the coming days. The district collector has directed local block development officers and municipal commissioners to monitor the distribution process personally to ensure equitable water sharing. For now, the people of Dharmapuri remain on high alert, praying for early monsoon showers to replenish the parched Hogenakkal reservoir and restore normalcy to their lives.
