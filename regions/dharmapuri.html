தர்மபுரி மாவட்டத்தின் கிராமப்புறப் பகுதிகள் முதல் நகர்ப்புறப் பகுதிகள் வரை பரவி வரும் இந்த கஞ்சா கலாச்சாரம், பெற்றோர்களிடையே மிகக் கடுமையான அச்சத்தையும் கவலையையும் ஏற்படுத்தியிருந்தது. குறிப்பாக, பள்ளி மற்றும் கல்லூரிக்குச் செல்லும் இளம் வயதினர் இந்த போதைப்பாதைக்குள் எளிதில் ஈர்க்கப்பட்டு, தங்களது வாழ்வையும் கல்வி எதிர்காலத்தையும் தொலைக்கும் அவல நிலை நீடித்து வந்தது. இந்த நிலையில், காவல்துறையினர் எடுத்துள்ள இந்த அதிரடி நடவடிக்கையானது போதைப்பொருள் கடத்தல் நெட்வொர்க்கை உடைத்தெறிந்துள்ளதுடன், மாவட்டத்தின் சட்டம் ஒழுங்கை பாதுகாப்பதில் முக்கியப் பங்காற்றியுள்ளது. இந்த கைது நடவடிக்கையின் மூலம் தர்மபுரியில் கஞ்சா புழக்கம் கணிசமாகக் குறையும் என்றும், பொதுமக்களின் பாதுகாப்பான வாழ்விட சூழல் உறுதி செய்யப்படும் என்றும் சமூக ஆர்வலர்கள் நம்பிக்கை தெரிவித்துள்ளனர்.

இந்த போதைப்பொருள் ஒழிப்புப் பணியில் பொதுமக்களின் ஒத்துழைப்பும் மிக அவசியமானது என்று மாவட்ட காவல்துறை வேண்டுகோள் விடுத்துள்ளது. தர்மபுரி மாவட்டத்தின் எந்தவொரு பகுதியிலும் கஞ்சா விற்பனையோ அல்லது போதைப்பொருள் கடத்தலோ நடைபெறுவது தெரியவந்தால், பொதுமக்கள் உடனடியாக காவல் கட்டுப்பாட்டு அறைக்கோ அல்லது ரகசிய எண்களுக்கோ தகவல் தெரிவிக்கலாம் என்று கேட்டுக்கொள்ளப்பட்டுள்ளது. தகவல் தருபவர்களின் விவரங்கள் முற்றிலும் ரகசியமாக வைக்கப்படும் என்றும், அவர்களின் பாதுகாப்பிற்கு முழு உத்தரவாதம் அளிக்கப்படும் என்றும் மாவட்ட காவல்துறை உறுதியளித்துள்ளது. காவல்துறையின் இந்த தொடர் கண்காணிப்பு மற்றும் அதிரடி நடவடிக்கைகள் மூலம் தர்மபுரி மாவட்டத்தில் கஞ்சா புழக்கம் முற்றிலும் வேரறுக்கப்படும் என்று எதிர்பார்க்கப்படுகிறது.

In a major crackdown against the illicit drug trade, the Dharmapuri district police have arrested 23 individuals in connection with various ganja smuggling and peddling cases over a span of just three days. This intensive drive was launched following specific instructions from senior police officials to curb the rising menace of narcotics among the local youth. The law enforcement agencies conducted coordinated raids across multiple jurisdictions within the district, targeting known hotspots and tracking suspicious movements. During these operations, officers seized significant quantities of contraband intended for local distribution, dealing a heavy blow to the regional drug supply chain.

The sudden surge in police action comes amid growing concerns over the infiltration of banned substances into rural and semi-urban pockets of Dharmapuri. Investigating officers revealed that the arrested individuals were part of a loosely organized network sourcing the contraband from neighboring regions to sell to school and college students. By targeting educational hubs and local recreational spots, these peddlers had been trying to establish a steady consumer base, prompting immediate intervention from the district administration. The police have registered multiple cases under the Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances (NDPS) Act, ensuring that those apprehended will face stringent legal consequences.

The local residents and community leaders have widely welcomed this decisive action, expressing relief over the proactive stance taken by the police department. For months, neighborhoods in Dharmapuri had witnessed an unsettling rise in anti-social activities, which residents directly linked to the unchecked availability of ganja. Parents and educators had previously voiced their anxieties regarding the safety of young youngsters who were increasingly vulnerable to these predatory drug peddlers. The success of this three-day drive has restored a sense of security among the public, who are now hopeful that such sustained efforts will permanently clean up their neighborhoods.

Responding to the successful operation, senior police officials of Dharmapuri district emphasized that this crackdown is not a one-time event but part of a larger, long-term strategy to eradicate drug abuse. The department has established dedicated surveillance teams to monitor transit routes and checkposts bordering adjacent districts to block the inflow of narcotics. Furthermore, the authorities have appealed to the general public to actively cooperate with the police by sharing confidential information regarding any suspicious activities in their respective localities. Officials have assured citizens that the identity of informants will be kept strictly confidential to encourage community participation in this fight against drugs.

Looking ahead, the district administration plans to complement these law enforcement measures with awareness campaigns targeting vulnerable demographics across Dharmapuri. Educational institutions will host interactive sessions to educate students about the severe legal and health hazards associated with drug consumption. Meanwhile, the police department is preparing to fast-track the legal proceedings against the 23 arrested individuals to send a strong deterrent message to other offenders. Through this dual approach of strict policing and community-level sensitization, Dharmapuri aims to foster a safer, drug-free environment for its future generations.

Several factors have contributed to this sudden downturn in water availability, chief among them being the lack of adequate seasonal rainfall in the catchment areas feeding the Cauvery River. This environmental strain is compounded by aging infrastructure and distribution losses along the transit pipelines. Engineers from the Tamil Nadu Water Supply and Drainage (TWAD) Board have noted that without a significant spell of rain in the upper riparian states, replenishing the reservoir to operational levels remains an uphill task.

Suggestions have been made to desilt existing water bodies in Dharmapuri and invest in rainwater harvesting systems to recharge the groundwater table. As climate variability makes monsoon patterns increasingly unpredictable, the district's reliance on a single major source like Hogenakkal is being questioned by environmentalists. The impact of this water scarcity is being felt acutely across several residential colonies and villages throughout Dharmapuri, where tap water supply has become highly irregular. As the hot weather continues to intensify across the region, the district administration is under immense pressure to restore regular supply before the situation escalates into a full-blown public protest. Erratic Hogenakkal water supply causes major distress to residents in Tamil Nadu's Dharmapuri

The landmark Hogenakkal Drinking Water Fluorosis Mitigation Project, designed to provide safe and clean drinking water to the chronically parched regions of Dharmapuri district, is currently failing to meet the basic needs of local residents due to highly erratic supply. Across several residential areas in the district, citizens are expressing deep anguish over the unpredictable distribution schedules, which have left them without adequate water for days on end. What was once hailed as a permanent solution to the region's severe groundwater fluorosis crisis has now become a source of daily anxiety for thousands of households. Local communities are forced to wait indefinitely for the municipal taps to run, disrupting their daily routines and throwing domestic life into complete disarray.

The root cause of the current crisis lies in a combination of deteriorating distribution infrastructure, frequent pipeline leakages, and operational inefficiencies within the local administrative machinery. Residents point out that the main transmission lines from the Hogenakkal intake point frequently suffer from pressure drops and technical snags, which are rarely addressed with any sense of urgency. Furthermore, the lack of a standardized and publicly communicated supply timetable has exacerbated the situation, leaving households unable to plan their water storage effectively. Despite the abundance of water at the source point of the Cauvery River, the local distribution network in Dharmapuri remains plagued by maintenance bottlenecks that prevent the water from reaching the end consumers consistently.

The impact on the daily lives of the residents in Dharmapuri has been profoundly disruptive, forcing many to find expensive alternatives to meet their basic survival needs. Families are increasingly forced to rely on private water tankers, which have capitalized on the artificial scarcity by charging exorbitant rates that middle and lower-income households can ill afford. Women, who traditionally bear the burden of managing household water requirements, are spending sleepless nights waiting for the taps to trickle, often to be disappointed by low-pressure supply that barely fills a few pots. This ongoing water insecurity has not only strained family budgets but has also raised fears of a resurgence in the use of local groundwater, which is notoriously contaminated with high levels of fluoride.

In response to the growing public outcry and mounting protests from civic groups, local administrative officials and representatives from the Tamil Nadu Water Supply and Drainage (TWAD) Board have acknowledged the distribution bottlenecks. Officials have stated that the irregular supply is primarily due to ongoing maintenance work, power fluctuations at the pumping stations, and the need to replace aging pipelines in specific sectors of the district. They have assured the public that dedicated technical teams are working to rectify the pressure discrepancies and seal existing leaks along the main distribution lines. Additionally, local authorities have promised to establish a more transparent and reliable supply schedule to ensure that all neighborhoods receive their fair share of water.

As the summer months approach, the pressure on the local administration to resolve this crisis permanently is mounting rapidly. Residents and social activists in Dharmapuri are demanding not just temporary fixes, but a comprehensive audit of the entire Hogenakkal water distribution network to identify systemic vulnerabilities. They argue that without a robust maintenance framework and strict accountability for municipal staff, the multi-crore project will fail to deliver on its primary promise of safeguarding public health. Until these structural reforms are implemented and consistent water supply is restored, the people of Dharmapuri remain trapped in a frustrating cycle of scarcity and administrative apathy.
