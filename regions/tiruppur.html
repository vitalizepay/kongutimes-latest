திருப்பூர் மாவட்டம் பல்லடம் அருகே உள்ள அலகுமலையில், சென்னை உயர் நீதிமன்ற வழக்கறிஞர் கொலை வழக்கின் முக்கிய சாட்சியான ரத்தினசாமி என்பவர் மர்ம நபர்களால் மிகக் கொடூரமான முறையில் வெட்டிப் படுகொலை செய்யப்பட்டுள்ளார். கடந்த 2021-ஆம் ஆண்டு சென்னை உயர் நீதிமன்ற வழக்கறிஞர் சிவக்குமார் என்பவர் நிலத்தகராறு காரணமாக ஒரு கும்பலால் படுகொலை செய்யப்பட்டார். இந்த வழக்கில் பல்லடத்தை அடுத்த அலகுமலை பகுதியைச் சேர்ந்த ரத்தினசாமி (வயது 52) என்பவர் மிக முக்கியமான அரசுத் தரப்பு சாட்சியாக இருந்து வந்தார். நீதிமன்றத்தில் இந்த வழக்கின் விசாரணை இறுதிக்கட்டத்தை எட்டியுள்ள நிலையில், சாட்சியம் அளிக்கவிருந்த ரத்தினசாமி படுகொலை செய்யப்பட்டுள்ளது திருப்பூர் மாவட்ட காவல் துறையினரிடையே பெரும் அதிர்ச்சியை ஏற்படுத்தியுள்ளது.

இந்தக் கொடூரச் சம்பவம் பல்லடம் காவல் எல்லைக்குட்பட்ட அலகுமலை பகுதியில் அரங்கேறியுள்ளது. ரத்தினசாமி தனது விவசாய நிலத்தில் வழக்கமான பணிகளில் ஈடுபட்டிருந்தபோது, அங்கு இருசக்கர வாகனங்களில் வந்த அடையாளம் தெரியாத ஆயுதம் ஏந்திய கும்பல் ஒன்று அவரைச் சூழ்ந்து கொண்டது. தப்பியோட முயன்ற ரத்தினசாமியை ஓட ஓட விரட்டி, அரிவாள் மற்றும் கத்திகளால் உடல் முழுவதும் கொடூரமான முறையில் அந்த கும்பல் வெட்டியுள்ளது. இதில் ரத்த வெள்ளத்தில் சரிந்த அவர் சம்பவ இடத்திலேயே பரிதாபமாக உயிரிழந்தார். கொலைக் கும்பல் அங்கிருந்து தப்பியோடிய நிலையில், அலகுமலை பகுதி மக்கள் இந்த பயங்கர சம்பவத்தைக் கண்டு பெரும் பீதியடைந்துள்ளனர்.

இந்த அதிர்ச்சிப் படுகொலைக்கான காரணம் குறித்துப் போலீஸார் நடத்திய முதற்கட்ட விசாரணையில், வழக்கறிஞர் சிவக்குமார் கொலை வழக்கின் சாட்சியத்தைக் கலைப்பதற்காகவே இந்த கொலை அரங்கேற்றப்பட்டுள்ளது என்பது தெரியவந்துள்ளது. சிவக்குமார் கொலை வழக்கில் குற்றம் சாட்டப்பட்ட நபர்கள், தங்களுக்கு எதிராக நீதிமன்றத்தில் சாட்சியம் அளிக்கக் கூடாது என்று ரத்தினசாமியைப் பலமுறை மிரட்டி வந்ததாகக் கூறப்படுகிறது. ஆனால், ரத்தினசாமி அதற்குப் பணியாமல் நீதிமன்றத்தில் சாட்சியம் அளிக்க உறுதியாக இருந்ததால், அவரைத் திட்டமிட்டு தீர்த்துக் கட்ட இந்தச் சதிச் செயல் அரங்கேற்றப்பட்டுள்ளது. பழிவாங்கும் நோக்கிலும், வழக்கிலிருந்து தப்பித்துக் கொள்ளும் நோக்கிலும் இந்தத் துணிகரக் கொலைச் சம்பவம் நடத்தப்பட்டுள்ளது.

A shocking wave of fear has gripped the industrial hub of Tiruppur following a brutal daylight attack on a crucial eyewitness associated with the high-profile murder of a Madras High Court advocate. The victim, who had been key to the ongoing investigation, was intercepted and hacked by an unidentified gang armed with lethal weapons in broad daylight. The sheer audacity of the assault, carried out in a busy public area, has sent shockwaves through the local community, raising immediate concerns over the safety of those who cooperate with law enforcement. Emergency services rushed to the scene upon receiving alerts from panicked onlookers, and the severely injured victim was immediately transported to a nearby government hospital for urgent medical intervention.

Local residents and business owners in the vicinity of the incident have expressed deep anxiety over the deteriorating law and order situation in the city. Many shopkeepers chose to close their establishments early in the wake of the violence, fearing retaliatory attacks or further disruptions in the locality. Community activists in Tiruppur are now demanding heightened security measures, pointing out that such brazen acts of violence diminish public trust in the witness protection framework. The atmosphere remains highly tense as citizens grapple with the reality of such extreme violence occurring in their everyday surroundings, urging the administration to take decisive action to restore peace.

Preliminary investigations suggest that the attack was a calculated attempt to silence the eyewitness and derail the judicial proceedings of the advocate's murder case. By targeting a individual central to the prosecution, the perpetrators aimed to intimidate other potential witnesses and weaken the legal case against the primary accused. Tiruppur district police have cordoned off the area where the assault took place, gathering forensic evidence and reviewing closed-circuit television footage from neighboring buildings to identify the assailants. Investigators believe the attackers had been tracking the victim's movements for several days prior to executing their violent plan.

In response to the public outcry, senior police officials in Tiruppur have deployed special teams to track down the fleeing suspects and prevent any further escalation of violence. The district administration has reassured the public that additional security personnel have been stationed at key junctions to maintain order and prevent any retaliatory incidents. A senior police spokesperson stated that the department is treating the case with the utmost priority, promising that those responsible for the attack will be apprehended swiftly and face the full force of the law. Measures are also being put in place to review and strengthen the security protocols for other vital witnesses connected to the sensitive murder trial.

This disturbing incident has sparked a wider conversation among legal professionals and civil society members in the region regarding the urgent need for robust witness protection programs at the district level. Legal experts emphasize that without guaranteed safety for individuals who come forward to assist the judiciary, prosecuting grave offenses becomes an uphill task for the state. As the victim battles for survival in the hospital, the local community remains on edge, hoping for a swift resolution to the crisis and a visible reassurance from the government that public safety in Tiruppur will not be compromised by criminal elements.

In a significant development that has gained widespread attention across the western belt of Tamil Nadu, agricultural associations in Palladam, Tiruppur district, have officially urged the state government to lift the long-standing ban on toddy tapping and sales. During a high-profile meeting convened in the agrarian hub of Palladam, representatives from various farmers' unions collectively voiced their grievances regarding the economic hardships they currently face. The farmers argued that the decades-old prohibition on this natural beverage has severely crippled the local rural economy, which heavily relies on coconut and palmyra cultivation. They formally petitioned the state administration, including key political figures and Chief Minister M.K. Stalin, to treat toddy as an agricultural product rather than an intoxicating liquor, thereby granting them the constitutional right to harvest and sell it.

The primary catalyst behind this renewed demand is the severe financial distress gripping the coconut farming community of Tiruppur district. Agriculturalists explained that fluctuating market prices for copra, rising input costs, and the devastating impact of invasive pests have made traditional coconut farming highly unsustainable in recent years. By allowing the tapping of toddy, which is the fermented sap extracted from the flowers of coconut or palmyra palms, farmers would be able to secure a stable, daily source of alternative income to support their households. The organizers of the meet emphasized that while neighbouring states like Kerala have successfully regulated and commercialized toddy to benefit their agrarian sectors, Tamil Nadu's persistent ban only pushes local farmers deeper into debt and poverty.

The impact of this ongoing prohibition is deeply felt by the rural residents and agricultural labourers of the Tiruppur region, who have seen their traditional livelihoods rapidly erode. Local farm workers, who once possessed specialized skills in palm climbing and sap extraction, have been forced to migrate to urban centres to work as daily-wage labourers in Tiruppur’s textile units. Residents of Palladam and surrounding villages note that the ban has not prevented the consumption of alcohol but has instead forced rural consumers toward expensive, state-run retail outlets selling Indian-Made Foreign Liquor (IMFL). Community leaders argue that lifting the ban would not only revive ancient rural craftsmanship but also provide local consumers with a natural, unadulterated beverage, free from the chemical additives often found in commercial alcohol.

Despite the growing intensity of the farmers' protests and their structured representations in Palladam, official responses from the state government and district administration remain cautious and non-committal. Senior administrative officials in Tiruppur have acknowledged receipt of the farmers' memorandum but have indicated that any decision regarding the amendment of the Tamil Nadu Prohibition Act, 1937, rests entirely with the state cabinet and policy-makers in Chennai. Revenue and excise department officials have expressed concerns over potential challenges in monitoring purity, regulating sales outlets, and preventing the illicit adulteration of toddy if the ban is lifted. However, local authorities have promised to forward the detailed economic feasibility reports and the farmers' demands to the higher state-level committees for a comprehensive review.

As the debate intensifies, the farming community in Tiruppur has warned of escalating their democratic agitations if the government continues to ignore their pleas. Agricultural unions are currently planning a series of peaceful demonstrations and awareness campaigns across the district to garner wider public support for their cause. They maintain that the revitalization of the rural economy in the Kongu region depends heavily on progressive agricultural policies that empower self-reliance. With the economic survival of thousands of families hanging in the balance, all eyes are now on the state government to see if it will yield to the persistent demands of the Tiruppur farmers and usher in a new era of agricultural reform.

A tense standoff erupted at the Tiruppur City Municipal Corporation ordinary council meeting on Friday as elected representatives from rival political parties clashed over the display of national and state leaders' portraits. The confrontation began when opposition councillors, primarily belonging to the All India Anna Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (AIADMK) and the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP), strongly objected to what they claimed was the selective display of portraits inside the council hall. The opposition members demanded that if portraits of ruling Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (DMK) leaders and former Chief Ministers were to be prominently displayed, the portraits of other prominent national leaders and late state leaders must also be given equal prominence. The verbal altercation quickly escalated, with councillors from both sides raising slogans against one another, temporarily bringing the civic body's proceedings to a complete standstill.

The root of the controversy lies in the strict protocol surrounding the decoration of government buildings and council halls, which has frequently become a flashpoint for regional political rivalry in Tamil Nadu. Opposition members argued that the ruling DMK administration was utilizing the municipal council hall to project its own party ideology by prioritizing specific portraits while ignoring established conventions. On the other hand, the ruling party councillors defended the current arrangement, asserting that the portraits displayed were in full compliance with state government guidelines and represented leaders who had made monumental contributions to the state's progress. As tempers flared, councillors gathered in the well of the house, exchanging heated arguments and stalling the scheduled legislative agenda for a significant portion of the morning session.

This political drama unfolding inside the corporation hall has drawn sharp criticism from the residents of Tiruppur, who feel that their pressing civic grievances are being sidelined by partisan politics. The industrial hub of Tiruppur is currently grappling with critical infrastructure issues, including delayed underground drainage works, irregular drinking water supply in several wards, and the poor maintenance of roads damaged by recent rains. He urged the councillors to cooperate in conducting the meeting smoothly so that crucial resolutions concerning the city's development could be passed without further delay. Following the official assurance that the opposition's representation would be forwarded to the state government for review, the protesting councillors relented, allowing the meeting to resume.

Despite the restoration of order, the incident has highlighted the deep political polarization within the Tiruppur Corporation council, raising concerns about the smooth functioning of future administrative sessions. Observers note that such frequent disruptions hinder the decision-making process for vital urban development projects in the dollar-earning garment city. While the council eventually managed to pass several pending resolutions later in the day, the shadow of the portrait row lingered over the proceedings. Civic activists have called for a permanent guidelines framework to be established for the council hall to prevent such ideological clashes from disrupting local governance in the future.

A tragic incident of domestic violence culminating in a murder-suicide has sent shockwaves through the industrial hub of Tiruppur, Tamil Nadu. A young man reportedly murdered his pregnant wife before taking his own life inside their residence, leaving the local community in deep mourning and disbelief. The deceased have been identified as 24-year-old Naveen and his 21-year-old wife, Arthi, who was several months pregnant at the time of the incident. The couple, who had been married for only a year, resided in a rental home within the city limits under the jurisdiction of the local police. Neighbors grew suspicious when the house remained locked for an unusually long period, prompting them to alert the authorities who subsequently made the gruesome discovery.

Preliminary investigations conducted by the Tiruppur police suggest that the extreme step was the result of escalating domestic disputes between the couple. Naveen, who worked as a laborer in one of Tiruppur's prominent garment units, had reportedly been facing financial difficulties, which exacerbated the frequent arguments at home. On the fateful night, a heated altercation broke out between the two, which tragically escalated to physical violence. In a fit of rage, Naveen allegedly strangled his pregnant wife to death. Realizing the gravity of his actions and overcome by panic and guilt, he then decided to end his own life by hanging himself in the same room.

The double tragedy has deeply impacted the residents of the neighborhood, who described the couple as generally quiet and reserved. Many neighbors expressed profound shock, noting that while minor disagreements are common in households, no one anticipated such a violent outcome, especially given Arthi’s pregnancy. Local community leaders have voiced concerns over the rising mental health pressures and financial stress among young working-class couples in the textile city. Neighbors gathered in large numbers outside the home as the police conducted their initial inquiries, with many calling for better community support systems and counseling services for young families facing domestic distress.

Upon receiving the alert, a team of police officials from the local station rushed to the spot, secured the scene, and recovered both bodies. The bodies were subsequently sent to the Tiruppur Government Hospital for a comprehensive post-mortem examination to ascertain the exact cause and time of death. Forensic experts were also summoned to the house to collect fingerprint samples and other physical evidence to rule out any external foul play. The police have registered a case under relevant sections of the law and have initiated a thorough investigation, which includes questioning family members, friends, and neighbors to understand the sequence of events leading up to the tragedy.

Revenue Divisional Officer (RDO) inquiry has also been initiated, as is mandatory under Indian law for cases where a woman dies under suspicious circumstances within seven years of marriage. Senior police officials in Tiruppur have urged the public, especially young couples experiencing severe domestic or financial stress, to seek assistance from counseling centers or the police helpline rather than taking extreme measures. This devastating incident has once again highlighted the urgent need for accessible mental health support and domestic conflict resolution mechanisms within the fast-paced industrial corridors of Tiruppur.
