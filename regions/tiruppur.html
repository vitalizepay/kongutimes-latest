திருப்பூர் மாவட்டம் பல்லடம் அருகே உள்ள அருள்புரம் பகுதியில், 4 மாத கர்ப்பிணிப் பெண் ஒருவர் அவரது கணவராலேயே அடித்துக் கொலை செய்யப்பட்ட கொடூர சம்பவம் அப்பகுதி மக்களிடையே பெரும் அதிர்ச்சியையும் சோகத்தையும் ஏற்படுத்தியுள்ளது. திண்டுக்கல் மாவட்டத்தைச் சேர்ந்த தம்பதியினர் பிழைப்பு தேடி திருப்பூர் வந்து, இங்குள்ள பின்னலாடை நிறுவனத்தில் பணியாற்றி வந்த நிலையில் இந்த விபரீதம் நிகழ்ந்துள்ளது. குடும்பத் தகராறு காரணமாக ஏற்பட்ட வாக்குவாதம், இறுதியில் ஒரு உயிரைப் பறிக்கும் அளவிற்குச் சென்றுள்ளது திருப்பூரின் தொழிலாளர் குடியிருப்புகளில் உள்ள பாதுகாப்பற்ற சூழலை மீண்டும் வெளிச்சத்திற்குக் கொண்டு வந்துள்ளது.

கொலை செய்யப்பட்ட பெண் சுமதி (பெயர் மாற்றப்பட்டுள்ளது) என்பதும், இவரைக் கொலை செய்த குற்றத்திற்காக அவரது கணவர் கார்த்திக் என்பவரைப் பல்லடம் காவல் துறையினர் கைது செய்துள்ளனர் என்பதும் முதற்கட்ட விசாரணையில் தெரியவந்துள்ளது. இவர்களுக்குத் திருமணமாகி சில ஆண்டுகளே ஆகும் நிலையில், குடும்பத்தில் அடிக்கடி பணப் பற்றாக்குறை மற்றும் தனிப்பட்ட காரணங்களால் தகராறு ஏற்பட்டு வந்துள்ளது. சம்பவத்தன்று இரவு இருவருக்கும் இடையே ஏற்பட்ட வாக்குவாதம் முற்றவே, ஆத்திரமடைந்த கார்த்திக் அருகில் இருந்த மரக்கட்டையால் சுமதியைத் தாக்கியுள்ளார். இதில் பலத்த காயமடைந்த அந்தப் பெண், கர்ப்பிணி என்றும் பாராமல் கொடூரமாகத் தாக்கப்பட்டு சம்பவ இடத்திலேயே பரிதாபமாக உயிரிழந்தார்.

இச்சம்பவம் குறித்துத் தகவல் அறிந்து விரைந்து வந்த பல்லடம் காவல் துறையினர், உயிரிழந்த பெண்ணின் உடலைக் கைப்பற்றிப் பிரேதப் பரிசோதனைக்காகத் திருப்பூர் அரசு மருத்துவமனைக்கு அனுப்பி வைத்தனர். மேலும், தப்பியோட முயன்ற கணவன் கார்த்திக்கை உடனடியாகக் கைது செய்து தீவிர விசாரணை மேற்கொண்டு வருகின்றனர். இத்தகைய குடும்ப வன்முறைகளைத் தடுக்கவும், தம்பதியினரிடையே ஏற்படும் மோதல்களைத் தீர்க்கவும் தகுந்த விழிப்புணர்வு மற்றும் சட்ட நடவடிக்கைகள் முன்னெடுக்கப்படும் என்று காவல் துறை அதிகாரிகள் தரப்பில் தெரிவிக்கப்பட்டுள்ளது.

In a tragic incident that has sent shockwaves across the industrial hub of Tiruppur, a 28-year-old man was arrested by the local police for allegedly murdering his 22-year-old wife, who was four months pregnant. The gruesome crime took place in their rented residence located in the heavily populated neighborhood of Nallur within the Tiruppur corporation limits. According to police sources, the victim was identified as Nandhini, while her husband was identified as Karthik, an industrial worker employed in one of the local garment exporting units. The discovery of the young woman's lifeless body has cast a grim shadow over the local community, raising serious concerns regarding domestic safety and the rising instances of spousal violence in the district.

Preliminary investigations conducted by the Tiruppur North police division revealed that the couple had been married for just over a year and were expecting their first child together. However, their domestic life was reportedly marred by frequent altercations, primarily driven by suspicion and financial distress. On the night of the incident, a heated argument broke out between Karthik and Nandhini regarding a domestic dispute, which quickly escalated into physical violence. In a fit of rage, Karthik allegedly assaulted his pregnant wife brutally and strangled her to death before fleeing the spot, leaving her body on the floor of their house.

The tragic event came to light the following morning when neighbors noticed an unusual silence emanating from the house and decided to investigate further. Upon discovering Nandhini lying unresponsive, they immediately alerted the local police and emergency medical services, who rushed to the scene only to declare her dead on arrival. The body of the deceased woman was subsequently shifted to the Tiruppur Government Hospital for a comprehensive post-mortem examination to determine the exact cause of death. The news of a pregnant woman being murdered by her own husband spread rapidly through the residential colony, drawing a large crowd of shocked onlookers and grieving relatives to the spot.

The brutal murder has deeply disturbed the residents of Tiruppur, with community leaders and women's rights activists expressing deep outrage over the vulnerability of women within their domestic spaces. Neighbors described Nandhini as a quiet, gentle woman who was eagerly looking forward to motherhood, making the nature of her death even more heartbreaking for the local populace. Many residents in the locality have called for stricter vigilance and better community-level support systems to address domestic abuse before it escalates to such fatal levels. The incident has also triggered intense discussions among the working-class families of the textile city regarding the psychological toll of financial stress and the urgent need for counseling services.

Responding swiftly to the heinous crime, the Tiruppur police formed special teams to track down the accused, who had gone into hiding shortly after committing the murder. Following a coordinated search operation utilizing local intelligence and mobile tracking, Karthik was apprehended by the police near the city outskirts within a few hours of the crime being reported. A senior police official confirmed that a case has been registered under Section 302 (murder) of the Indian Penal Code, and the accused has been remanded to judicial custody after being produced before a local magistrate. The district administration and the police department have assured the public that a thorough investigation will be conducted to ensure that justice is served swiftly for the deceased woman and her unborn child.

This massive ecological project, designed to divert surplus water from the Bhavani River to replenish depleted aquifers, has successfully breathed new life into thousands of parched farm wells and borewells across the region. For decades, local farmers in the dry belt of Avinashi and surrounding areas in Tiruppur had struggled with severe water scarcity, which forced many to abandon their ancestral agricultural lands. With the systematic implementation of this mega-irrigation initiative, the regional groundwater table has shown a remarkable rise, ushering in a wave of hope for the local farming community.

Despite the widespread celebration surrounding the project's overall success, several critical operational challenges have emerged, threatening to disrupt its long-term benefits for the residents of Tiruppur. Local farmers and agricultural union representatives have pointed out that while some areas are receiving ample water, several tail-end feed ponds and local lakes remain dry due to technical snags in the pipeline distribution network. The uneven distribution of water, coupled with occasional pressure drops in the pumping stations, has left many agrarian families in the interior pockets of the district frustrated. These residents argue that the full potential of this life-changing scheme can only be realized if the water reaches every designated water body without any geographical discrimination.

The primary cause of these operational hiccups has been attributed to minor alignment errors in the underground pipeline network and occasional power supply fluctuations at the main pumping stations. Farmers have urged the authorities to conduct a comprehensive audit of the pipeline routes to identify blocks and ensure that the gravity-fed lines function at maximum efficiency. Additionally, there are growing concerns regarding the maintenance of the newly constructed infrastructure, as some local water bodies have not been properly desilted to hold the incoming water. Agriculturalists and coconut growers in the Palladam region of Tiruppur district have intensified their long-standing demand for the state government to lift the ban on toddy tapping and sales. During a recent association meeting held in Palladam, local farmers collectively urged the Tamil Nadu government to permit the extraction of this natural beverage, which they argue is a traditional agricultural right. The farmers highlighted that while neighboring states like Kerala successfully regulate and promote toddy as a natural health drink, Tamil Nadu continues to enforce a decades-old prohibition that severely impacts the rural economy. Local community leaders expressed that lifting the ban would provide a sustainable livelihood for thousands of families dependent on coconut and palmyra cultivation in the drought-prone blocks of the district.

The primary driver behind this renewed agitation is the severe financial distress currently gripping the coconut farming sector in Tiruppur. Farmers are facing a double blow of plummeting prices for raw coconuts and copra, alongside a devastating whitefly infestation that has drastically reduced crop yields across the region. By allowing the tapping of toddy directly from coconut trees, agriculturalists believe they can diversify their income streams and survive the ongoing market slump. Representatives from various farmers' associations pointed out that toddy is a perishable, natural sap with low alcoholic content, making it fundamentally different from synthetic liquors sold through state-run outlets. They argue that the current policy forces them to witness their crops go to waste while they struggle to pay off agricultural loans.

The impact of the ongoing ban is deeply felt by the local residents and landless agricultural laborers of Tiruppur, who have seen their employment opportunities shrink over the years. Traditionally, toddy tapping provided consistent, daily-wage employment to thousands of skilled climbers and rural workers who have now been forced to migrate to urban centers in search of low-paying industrial jobs. Local consumers and traditional medicine practitioners also support the farmers, noting that fresh, unfermented toddy, known locally as 'Neera', possesses immense nutritional value and health benefits. Residents argue that reviving this traditional industry would not only boost the rural economy of Palladam but also curb the menace of illicit liquor and cheap, low-quality spirits that pose a major health hazard to the working-class population.

In a strategic political move, the farmers' unions have decided to escalate their grievances to the newly formed political leadership in the state, specifically appealing to Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam (TVK) chief Vijay to champion their cause. By urging prominent political figures to include the lifting of the toddy ban in their official policy platforms, the agriculturalists of Tiruppur hope to exert maximum pressure on the ruling administration ahead of upcoming electoral cycles. The farmers emphasized that any political party genuine about rural welfare must support the legalisation of toddy, as it directly concerns the self-reliance of the state's farming community. They have warned of widespread democratic protests and boycotts across the western belt of Tamil Nadu if their demands continue to be ignored by policymakers.

While the district administration and state cooperative officials have acknowledged receipt of the farmers' petitions, a formal response regarding policy change remains pending from the higher echelons of the state government. Local officials from the agriculture department in Tiruppur stated that while they understand the economic pressures faced by the coconut growers, any decision regarding the amendment of the Tamil Nadu Prohibition Act of 1937 rests solely with the state cabinet. In the interim, authorities have promised to look into alternative relief measures, such as increasing the procurement of copra through government-run centers and providing subsidized pest control measures to combat the whitefly epidemic. However, the farmers of Palladam remain steadfast in their stance, declaring that temporary subsidies cannot replace their fundamental right to harvest and sell the natural produce of their own land.

In a stern warning to property owners in Tiruppur, the Municipal Commissioner has issued a critical notice regarding pending property tax payments, signaling a comprehensive enforcement drive aimed at recovering substantial outstanding municipal revenues. The Tiruppur Municipal Corporation is taking decisive action against property owners who have consistently neglected their tax obligations, potentially facing significant consequences for continued non-compliance. This development comes as part of the municipal administration's broader strategy to streamline revenue collection and ensure equitable financial management within the urban jurisdiction.

The municipal authorities have revealed that numerous property owners across Tiruppur district have accumulated considerable arrears in property tax payments, creating substantial financial strain on the local government's operational capabilities. Official records indicate that the cumulative unpaid property tax amounts to several crores of rupees, which could potentially fund critical urban infrastructure and developmental projects if collected effectively. The Commissioner's recent communication emphasizes the legal mechanisms available to the municipal corporation to recover these outstanding dues, including potential property attachments and legal proceedings against persistent defaulters.

Under the current municipal regulations, property owners are legally mandated to pay their annual property taxes within stipulated timeframes, with clear guidelines and multiple payment channels established to facilitate convenient transactions. The municipal administration has strategically designed a comprehensive system that allows digital and offline payment options, enabling residents to fulfill their tax obligations without undue hardship. However, repeated non-compliance has prompted the current stringent approach, wherein defaulters will face progressively severe consequences, including potential legal action and financial penalties that could significantly impact their property ownership rights.

The enforcement drive is expected to have far-reaching implications for Tiruppur's urban financial ecosystem. By systematically addressing tax delinquency, the municipal corporation aims to generate additional revenue streams that can be redirected towards critical municipal services, infrastructure development, and public welfare initiatives. Local government officials have emphasized that consistent tax collection is fundamental to maintaining and improving urban infrastructure, including road maintenance, water supply systems, sanitation services, and public health facilities that directly benefit the city's residents.

Residents and property owners in Tiruppur are strongly advised to verify their tax payment status and clear any pending dues immediately to avoid potential legal complications. The municipal commissioner has indicated that a comprehensive review of property tax records is underway, with advanced digital tracking mechanisms being employed to identify and pursue defaulters. Property owners can access their tax details and make payments through the municipal corporation's official website, designated bank branches, and municipal tax collection centers. The administration has also established a dedicated helpline and support system to assist residents in understanding their tax obligations and resolving any procedural complexities related to property tax payments.
