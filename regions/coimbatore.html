கோவை ஒருங்கிணைந்த நீதிமன்ற வளாகத்தில் உள்ள 5-ஆவது கூடுதல் மாவட்ட நீதிமன்றத்தில், இன்று (ஜூலை 6) நடைபெற்ற பல்வேறு வழக்கு விசாரணைகளின் போது அரசுத் தரப்பு வழக்கறிஞர்கள் ஆஜராகாததால் பெரும் பரபரப்பு ஏற்பட்டது. நீதிமன்றத்திற்கு சாட்சிகளும் குற்றஞ்சாட்டப்பட்டவர்களும் நேரில் வந்திருந்த நிலையிலும், அரசுத் தரப்பில் வாதாட வேண்டிய கூடுதல் அரசு வழக்கறிஞர்கள் வராததால் வழக்குகளை ஒத்திவைக்க வேண்டிய கட்டாயம் ஏற்பட்டது. இதனால் ஆத்திரமடைந்த நீதிபதி சிவக்குமார், நீதிமன்ற நேரத்தையும் சாட்சிகளின் நேரத்தையும் வீணடித்ததற்காக கோவை மாவட்ட ஆட்சியர் கிராந்தி குமார் பாடிக்கு (சார்பாக மாநில அரசுக்கு) மொத்தம் 8 வழக்குகளில் சேர்த்து ரூ. 62,000 அபராதம் விதித்து அதிரடி உத்தரவு பிறப்பித்தார். இந்த அபராதத் தொகையை ஒரு வார காலத்திற்குள் நீதிமன்றத்தில் செலுத்த வேண்டும் என்றும் அவர் தனது உத்தரவில் மிகத் தெளிவாகக் குறிப்பிட்டுள்ளார்.

கோவை மாவட்டம் கருமத்தம்பட்டி காவல் நிலைய எல்லைக்குட்பட்ட பகுதியில் நடைபெற்ற கொலை வழக்கு விசாரணை, கடந்த 10 ஆண்டுகளாக இந்த 5-ஆவது கூடுதல் நீதிமன்றத்தில் தொடர்ந்து நடைபெற்று வருகிறது. இந்த வழக்கின் முக்கியக் குற்றவாளி இன்று பலத்த பாதுகாப்புடன் நீதிமன்றத்தில் ஆஜர்படுத்தப்பட்டார். மேலும், இந்த வழக்கில் மிக முக்கிய சாட்சியம் அளிப்பதற்காக அரசு மருத்துவர் ஒருவர் அண்டை மாநிலமான கேரளாவிலிருந்து நீண்ட தூரம் பயணம் செய்து கோவை நீதிமன்றத்திற்கு வந்திருந்தார். ஆனால், அரசுத் தரப்பு கூடுதல் வழக்கறிஞர் நீதிமன்றத்தில் ஆஜராகாத காரணத்தால், கேரளாவிலிருந்து வந்த மருத்துவரிடம் சாட்சி விசாரணை நடத்த முடியாத அவல நிலை ஏற்பட்டது. தமிழக அரசுத் தரப்பின் இத்தகைய அலட்சியப் போக்கினால்தான் இந்த வழக்கை ஒத்திவைக்க வேண்டிய நிலை ஏற்பட்டுள்ளதாகக் குறிப்பிட்ட நீதிபதி சிவக்குமார், இதற்காக மாவட்ட ஆட்சியருக்கு ரூ. 10,000 அபராதம் விதித்ததோடு, இந்தத் தொகையை கேரளாவிலிருந்து வந்த மருத்துவரின் பயணச் செலவுக்காக வழங்க வேண்டும் என்றும் உத்தரவிட்டார்.

இதேபோன்று, கோவை பந்தயசாலை காவல் நிலையத்தில் பதிவு செய்யப்பட்ட மற்றொரு முக்கிய வழக்கும் இன்று நீதிமன்றத்தில் விசாரணைக்குப் பட்டியலிடப்பட்டிருந்தது. இந்த வழக்கில் சாட்சியம் அளிப்பதற்காகத் திருவாரூரிலிருந்து காவல் துறை உதவி ஆய்வாளர் ஒருவர் நீதிமன்றத்தில் ஆஜராகியிருந்தார். மேலும், இந்த வழக்கில் குற்றஞ்சாட்டப்பட்ட 5 நபர்களும் நீதிமன்றத்திற்கு நேரில் வந்திருந்தனர். ஆனால், இந்த வழக்கிலும் கூடுதல் அரசு வழக்கறிஞர் ஆஜராகாததால் எவ்வித விசாரணையும் நடைபெறாமல் முடங்கியது. அரசுத் தரப்பின் இந்தத் பொறுப்பற்ற செயலால், சாட்சியாக வந்த காவல் அதிகாரியும், குற்றஞ்சாட்டப்பட்டவர்களும் தேவையின்றி அலைக்கழிக்கப்பட்டு கடும் சிரமத்திற்கு உள்ளாகியுள்ளனர் என்று சுட்டிக்காட்டிய நீதிபதி, இதற்காக கோவை மாவட்ட ஆட்சியர் சார்பாக மாநில அரசு ரூ. 9,000 அபராதத் தொகையை நீதிமன்றத்தில் செலுத்த வேண்டும் என உத்தரவிட்டார். இந்தத் தொகையிலிருந்து குற்றஞ்சாட்டப்பட்ட 5 பேருக்கு தலா 1,000 ரூபாயும், திருவாரூரிலிருந்து வந்த காவல் துறை உதவி ஆய்வாளருக்கு 4,000 ரூபாயும் இழப்பீடாக வழங்க வேண்டும் என்று நீதிபதி தீர்ப்பளித்தார்.

இதேபோல் இன்று விசாரணைக்கு வந்த மொத்தம் 8 வழக்குகளிலும் அரசுத் தரப்பு வழக்கறிஞர்கள் ஆஜராகாத காரணத்தினால், நீதிமன்றத்தின் நேரம் வீணடிக்கப்பட்டது மட்டுமின்றி, சாட்சிகளாக வந்த பொதுமக்கள் மற்றும் அரசு அதிகாரிகள் கடும் மன உளைச்சலுக்கு ஆளாகினர். ஒவ்வொரு வழக்காக விசாரித்த நீதிபதி சிவக்குமார், அரசுத் தரப்பின் இந்தத் தொடர் அலட்சியப் போக்கைக் கடுமையாகக் கண்டித்து, 8 வழக்குகளுக்கும் சேர்த்து ஒட்டுமொத்தமாக ரூ. 62,000 அபராதம் விதித்தார். அரசுத் தரப்பு வழக்கறிஞர்களின் இத்தகைய முறையற்ற செயல்பாடுகளால் நீதிமன்றத்தின் அன்றாடப் பணிகள் பாதிக்கப்படுவதோடு, சாமானிய மக்களுக்கு நீதி கிடைப்பதிலும் கடும் தாமதம் ஏற்படுகிறது என்று வழக்கறிஞர்கள் வட்டாரத்திலும் சமூக ஆர்வலர்களிடையேயும் பெரும் அதிருப்தி எழுந்துள்ளது.

Coimbatore Court Fines District Collector Rs 62,000 As Government Advocates Fail To Appear

In a stern warning against bureaucratic apathy and administrative negligence, the Coimbatore Integrated Court Complex witnessed unprecedented scenes on Saturday, July 6, when the Fifth Additional District Court slapped a combined fine of Rs 62,000 on Coimbatore District Collector Kranthi Kumar Pati. The punitive action was taken by Judge Sivakumar after government prosecutors failed to appear for hearings in eight different cases scheduled for the day. This chronic absenteeism on the part of the prosecution brought the judicial proceedings to a grinding halt, causing immense frustration and waste of public resources. Legal experts in the city have noted that such a strong judicial reprimand against the district's highest administrative official is rare and reflects the court's growing impatience with systemic delays.

The first major lapse occurred during the hearing of a decade-old murder case originating from Karumathampatti, which has been dragging on in the Fifth Additional District Court for the last ten years. On Saturday, the accused was duly produced in court, and a medical doctor had traveled all the way from neighbouring Kerala to tender crucial witness testimony. However, because the Additional Public Prosecutor failed to show up, the court was unable to conduct the examination of the witness, forcing an unnecessary adjournment. Expressing strong displeasure at the state's failure, Judge Sivakumar noted that the delay was entirely due to the negligence of the prosecution and ordered the Coimbatore District Collector to pay a fine of Rs 10,000 within one week, directing that this amount be compensated to the doctor from Kerala to cover his travel expenses.

In another scheduled trial investigated by the Race Course Police, a similar situation unfolded, highlighting a pattern of systemic failure. An Assistant Sub-Inspector of Police had traveled a long distance from Tiruvarur to give evidence, and all five accused individuals in the case were also present in the courtroom. Once again, the prosecution's absence stalled the trial, prompting Judge Sivakumar to observe that both the witnesses and the accused were being subjected to unwarranted harassment and mental agony due to the state's lapse. In a significant boost to the social and civic infrastructure of Coimbatore, the Rotary Club has announced a comprehensive suite of new community service projects aimed at addressing key developmental needs across the district. This major welfare initiative, designed to uplift underprivileged sections of society and enhance public amenities, was officially unveiled during a high-profile regional meeting of the organisation's leadership. The newly launched projects will primarily focus on critical areas such as healthcare accessibility, educational support for government school students, environmental conservation, and clean drinking water facilities. By targeting these essential sectors, the organisation aims to bridge the gaps in existing public infrastructure and foster sustainable development throughout the industrial hub of Coimbatore.

The decision to launch these targeted initiatives stems from a detailed assessment of local community needs, particularly in the suburban and rural pockets of the district where access to quality healthcare and modern educational resources remains limited. To ensure the maximum impact of these welfare schemes, the Rotary Club has mobilised substantial financial resources and partnered with local administrative bodies, educational institutions, and healthcare providers. Among the flagship projects is the establishment of advanced medical equipment clinics and the upgrading of infrastructure in several government-run schools. Additionally, the club plans to install high-capacity water purification plants in neglected residential neighborhoods to ensure that clean, potable water is readily available to thousands of families, thereby reducing the incidence of waterborne diseases in the region.

The official launch event was attended by prominent civic leaders, district administrators, and senior Rotary officials, who expressed their strong commitment to the welfare of Coimbatore's residents. The project coordinators also detailed the implementation timeline, assuring the public that the execution of these initiatives would be closely monitored to ensure transparency and timely completion.

Local residents and community activists have reacted with immense optimism to the announcement, noting that these interventions will directly improve their daily lives and ease financial burdens. Families living in the outer areas of the city, who often have to travel long distances for basic medical check-ups, are particularly enthusiastic about the proposed mobile healthcare clinics and diagnostic camps. By focusing on root-level issues, the newly introduced welfare model is set to create a healthier, more educated, and resilient populace in the region.

As the implementation phase begins, the Rotary Club has established dedicated committees comprising local volunteers and technical experts to oversee the day-to-day progress of each project. District authorities have assured full administrative cooperation, including the granting of necessary permissions and logistical support, to facilitate the smooth rollout of the infrastructure work. This collaborative effort is expected to set a new benchmark for corporate social responsibility and civic engagement in Coimbatore, inspiring other organisations to invest in the district's social capital. With a clear roadmap and strong community backing, these new service projects are poised to drive meaningful progress and enhance the overall quality of life for the people of Coimbatore in the coming years.

The pristine and highly popular eco-tourism destination of Kovai Kutralam, nestled in the foot slopes of the Western Ghats near Siruvani in Coimbatore district, has been temporarily closed to the public and tourists. The decision was taken by the Coimbatore Forest Division following a sudden and heavy spell of rainfall in the catchment areas of the Western Ghats, which led to a significant and rapid rise in the water flow at the Kovai Kutralam falls. Forest officials noted that the safety of visitors is of paramount importance, and allowing tourists near the cascading waters under such volatile weather conditions could lead to hazardous situations.

According to local forest rangers and administrative officials, the region has been experiencing intermittent heavy downpours over the last forty-eight hours, causing the mountain streams that feed the main waterfall to swell rapidly. Fearing flash floods and slippery rocky terrains, the forest department immediately deployed staff to clear the bathing areas and officially announced the suspension of entry for tourists until further notice. This preemptive measure is a standard safety protocol implemented by the Coimbatore administration whenever the water levels cross the danger threshold, ensuring that no untoward incidents occur in the dense forest zone.

The sudden closure has had a direct impact on both local residents and travellers from neighbouring districts who flock to the scenic spot, especially during the weekends, to escape the city heat. Several disappointed tourists who had travelled long distances to visit the falls had to be turned back at the Sadivayal check post, which serves as the entry point to the eco-tourism site. Local vendors, small tea stall owners, and transport operators in the Alandurai and Sadivayal areas have also expressed concern over the temporary shutdown, as their daily livelihoods are heavily dependent on the steady influx of visitors to the waterfalls.

In response to the situation, senior officials from the Coimbatore Forest Division have issued a public advisory urging people to cooperate with the department and avoid planning trips to the forest area for the next few days. Forest guards have been stationed at key vantage points and entry gates to monitor the water levels continuously and to prevent any unauthorized entry into the reserve forest. Official sources indicated that a thorough assessment of the weather conditions and water flow will be conducted daily, and the waterfalls will be reopened to the public only after the water flow subsides to a completely safe level.

Residents of Coimbatore have largely welcomed the timely intervention by the authorities, acknowledging that flash floods in these forest streams can be highly unpredictable and dangerous. The district administration has also requested the public to stay updated through official government press releases rather than relying on unverified social media reports regarding the status of the tourist spot. With the monsoon patterns showing increased activity across the Western Ghats, the Coimbatore Forest Department remains on high alert, prioritizing ecological preservation and public safety above all else.

The construction sector in Coimbatore is staring at a major slowdown as the local brick kiln lorry owners have officially launched an indefinite strike, bringing the transport of essential building materials to a grinding halt. The strike, which commenced earlier this week, was called by the Coimbatore District Brick Kiln Lorry Owners Association to protest against rising operational costs and recent regulatory changes. Hundreds of heavy vehicles dedicated to transporting red bricks from local manufacturing units to various construction sites across the district have parked their fleets in solidarity. Association representatives state that the strike will continue indefinitely until their long-standing grievances are addressed by the district administration and relevant state departments.

The primary catalyst for this sudden industrial action is the steep hike in toll charges, fuel prices, and the stringent new regulations imposed on mining and transporting red soil, which is the primary raw material for brick manufacturing. Lorry owners argue that the soaring cost of diesel combined with increased vehicle maintenance charges has made their business model entirely unsustainable under current freight rates. Furthermore, they claim that local law enforcement and transport department officials have been imposing hefty fines for minor weight deviations, creating an atmosphere of constant harassment. The operators are demanding a streamlined single-window clearance system for mineral transport permits and a standardized, fair pricing structure that reflects the current economic realities of the transport industry.

The immediate fallout of this strike is being heavily felt across Coimbatore district, where real estate and infrastructure development form the backbone of the local economy. Major residential apartment projects, commercial complexes, and individual house construction sites in areas like Thudiyalur, Saravanampatti, and Peelamedu have already begun reporting a severe shortage of bricks. Contractors and builders express deep concern that a prolonged strike will not only delay project timelines but also escalate overall construction costs, a burden that will ultimately be passed on to homebuyers. Daily-wage construction laborers, who rely on a steady flow of materials to secure their daily employment, are also facing the sudden threat of job losses as work sites dry up.

Local residents who are currently building or renovating their homes have been caught in the crossfire of this industrial dispute, facing unexpected financial strain and logistical nightmares. Many individual homebuilders have reported that suppliers have already started quoting premium prices for existing stockpiles of bricks, leading to artificial inflation in the local market. Residents lament that their carefully planned construction schedules have been thrown into complete disarray, forcing them to extend temporary rental agreements and incur additional bank interest on their housing loans. The community is collectively urging both the striking transporters and the government authorities to reach a swift compromise before the local housing market suffers irreversible damage.

In response to the escalating crisis, senior officials from the Coimbatore district administration have initiated preliminary talks with the representatives of the Brick Kiln Lorry Owners Association to break the deadlock. A senior regional transport official stated that while the government is sensitive to the economic pressures faced by the transporters, compliance with road safety norms and mineral overloading regulations cannot be compromised. The administration has proposed a joint committee meeting comprising transport officials, mining department representatives, and association leaders to systematically review the grievances regarding fines and permit delays. As both sides prepare for the next round of negotiations, the residents and builders of Coimbatore anxiously await a resolution that will put the wheels of the local construction industry back in motion.

இது குறித்து மாவட்ட நிர்வாகம் மற்றும் மாநகராட்சி அதிகாரிகளிடம் கேட்டபோது, கோவையின் இழந்த நீர் வரலாற்றை மீட்டெடுக்கத் தீவிர நடவடிக்கைகள் மேற்கொள்ளப்பட்டு வருவதாகத் தெரிவித்தனர். விடுபட்ட மற்றும் ஆக்கிரமிக்கப்பட்ட நீர் வழிகளைக் கண்டறிந்து, அவற்றை முழுமையாக மீட்பதற்கான நில அளவைப் பணிகள் வருவாய்த் துறையினரின் உதவியுடன் முடுக்கிவிடப்பட்டுள்ளதாகக் குறிப்பிட்டனர். மேலும், நீர்நிலைகளை ஆக்கிரமித்துக் கட்டப்பட்டுள்ள கட்டிடங்களை அகற்றுவதற்கும், ஆக்கிரமிப்பாளர்கள் மீது கடுமையான சட்டப்பூர்வ நடவடிக்கை எடுப்பதற்கும் சிறப்புப் படைகள் அமைக்கப்பட்டு வருவதாக அதிகாரிகள் தரப்பில் உறுதியளிக்கப்பட்டுள்ளது.

கோவையின் பாரம்பரிய நீர் ஆதாரங்களைப் பாதுகாப்பது என்பது வெறும் அதிகாரிகளின் கடமை மட்டுமல்ல, அது பொதுமக்களின் கூட்டுப் பொறுப்பாகும் என்று சமூக ஆர்வலர்கள் வலியுறுத்துகின்றனர். மறைந்து போன நதிகளையும் ஓடைகளையும் மீட்டெடுக்காவிட்டால், எதிர்காலத்தில் கோவை மாநகரம் கடுமையான வறட்சியையும் சுற்றுச்சூழல் சமநிலையின்மையையும் சந்திக்க நேரிடும் என்று எச்சரிக்கப்படுகிறது. எனவே, அரசு உடனடியாகத் தலையிட்டு, நீர்நிலைகளின் எல்லைகளை வரையறுத்து, தூர்வாரி, கோவையின் மறைக்கப்பட்ட நீர் வரலாற்றிற்குப் புத்துயிர் ஊட்ட வேண்டும் என்பதே ஒட்டுமொத்த கோவை மக்களின் எதிர்பார்ப்பாக உள்ளது.

How Coimbatore Rivers Disappeared: The Hidden Water History of the City Revealed

The historic city of Coimbatore, often celebrated as the Manchester of South India, is currently grappling with a severe ecological crisis rooted in the systemic disappearance of its ancient natural water bodies and rivers. A detailed historical analysis reveals that the region, which once boasted a flourishing network of natural streams and tributaries feeding into the Noyyal River, has lost a significant portion of its water resources due to rapid, unplanned urbanization and industrial expansion over the last few decades. Environmental historians and local activists point out that several seasonal rivers and channels, which once acted as the primary source of groundwater recharge for the district, have been systematically erased from the geographical maps. This loss of natural blue infrastructure has transformed the once-water-abundant Kongu region into a zone highly vulnerable to acute water scarcity during the summer months and flash floods during the monsoon.

The primary drivers behind this environmental catastrophe are decades of unchecked encroachment, administrative apathy, and the conversion of natural water courses into residential layouts and industrial zones. As Coimbatore grew exponentially to accommodate its booming textile and manufacturing sectors, real estate developers and local planning bodies overlooked the vital paths of traditional water channels, building over them with impunity. Furthermore, the dumping of solid municipal waste and the direct discharge of untreated industrial effluents have choked the remaining active streams, turning once-pristine water sources into toxic drains. This severe degradation of the local hydrology has not only disrupted the natural flow of the Noyyal River basin but has also permanently severed the ecological links between the Western Ghats' foothills and the urban plains of Coimbatore.

The impact of this ecological destruction on the everyday lives of Coimbatore’s residents is profound and increasingly alarming. Many neighborhoods that previously enjoyed abundant groundwater at shallow depths are now forced to drill borewells hundreds of feet deep, only to find contaminated or completely dry aquifers. This widespread depletion of the water table has created a heavy reliance on private water tankers, significantly escalating the cost of living for middle-class families and marginalized communities alike. Agriculturalists on the fringes of the city, who historically depended on these lost tributaries for irrigation, have been forced to abandon their ancestral farmlands or shift to less water-intensive, less profitable crops, thereby threatening the region's overall food security and rural economy.

In response to growing public outrage and sustained campaigns by local non-governmental organizations like Siruthuli and various environmental coalitions, the district administration and the Coimbatore Municipal Corporation have initiated several restoration projects. Official spokespersons have stated that the government is actively working on the rejuvenation of the Noyyal River system and has commenced desilting operations in several surviving lakes under the Smart Cities Mission. However, civic activists argue that these cosmetic beautification efforts often prioritize recreational lakefronts over the actual restoration of the lost inlet and outlet channels that are crucial for the hydrological cycle. Officials have promised to conduct comprehensive drone surveys to identify historical water pathways and take strict legal action against commercial establishments encroaching on government-owned water bodies.

To prevent Coimbatore from sliding into an irreversible water crisis, environmental experts stress that the government must move beyond temporary fixes and implement a scientifically backed, community-led watershed management program. Restoring the lost rivers of the district requires a strong political will to evict powerful encroachers, alongside a massive public awareness campaign to reconnect the urban population with their natural heritage. Only by strictly enforcing zoning laws, establishing buffer zones around existing channels, and actively mapping the historical water courses can the administration hope to revive the lost blue veins of Coimbatore. Without these urgent corrective measures, the future of this premier industrial hub remains ecologically unsustainable, threatening the health and livelihood of generations to come.
