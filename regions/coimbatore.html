பயணிகளின் இந்த தொடர் கோரிக்கையை ஏற்று, தென்னக ரயில்வே நிர்வாகத்தின் உயர்மட்ட அதிகாரிகள் இந்த வார இறுதி சிறப்பு ரயில் சேவையை அறிமுகப்படுத்தியுள்ளனர். இந்த சிறப்பு ரயிலானது கோயம்புத்தூர் சந்திப்பு ரயில் நிலையத்திலிருந்து புறப்பட்டு திருப்பூர், ஈரோடு, சேலம் மற்றும் காட்பாடி வழியாக சென்னை சென்ட்ரல் ரயில் நிலையத்தை சென்றடையும் வகையில் வழித்தடம் அமைக்கப்பட்டுள்ளது. வெள்ளிக்கிழமை இரவு கோவையில் இருந்து புறப்படும் இந்த ரயில், சனிக்கிழமை அதிகாலை சென்னையை சென்றடையும். அதேபோல் ஞாயிற்றுக்கிழமை இரவு சென்னையிலிருந்து புறப்பட்டு திங்கட்கிழமை அதிகாலை கோவைக்கு வந்து சேரும் வகையில் இந்த ரயிலின் கால அட்டவணை திட்டமிடப்பட்டுள்ளது.

The Southern Railway's decision to operate special weekend trains between Coimbatore and Chennai has been met with widespread acclaim from the residents of the Kongu region. For months, passengers travelling between these two major economic hubs of Tamil Nadu faced immense difficulties due to the severe shortage of available berths on regular express trains. The demand peaks significantly on Friday evenings and Sunday nights as working professionals, students, and business travellers commute between their workplaces in Chennai and their hometowns in Coimbatore. This new initiative by the railway administration directly addresses this long-standing grievance by injecting much-needed passenger capacity during these high-volume peak periods.

According to railway officials, the special trains will run on select days of the week to specifically cater to the weekend rush that typically overwhelms the standard daily services. The decision was made after analyzing reservation waiting lists and receiving numerous representations from passenger welfare associations in Coimbatore who highlighted the plight of commuters forced to rely on expensive private omnibuses. By introducing these special services, the Southern Railway aims to provide a reliable, safe, and highly affordable alternative for the public. The train services are scheduled to operate with convenient timings, allowing passengers to complete their journeys overnight without disrupting their professional or academic commitments.

Local residents and regular commuters in Coimbatore have expressed immense relief and gratitude regarding the introduction of these special weekend trains. Many travellers noted that booking a ticket on the Kovai Express, Cheran Express, or the Shatabdi Express had become nearly impossible unless planned months in advance. Consequently, many were left at the mercy of private bus operators who frequently inflated their fares during weekends and festival seasons, sometimes charging double the standard rate. With the introduction of these special trains, middle-class families and students can now travel comfortably and budget-friendly, ensuring they can visit their families without facing financial strain or travel insecurity.

The Coimbatore District Passengers Welfare Association has welcomed the move but also urged the railway ministry to consider making these temporary weekend specials into permanent services. Representatives from the association pointed out that Coimbatore, as a rapidly growing industrial and educational powerhouse, generates a continuous flow of passenger traffic to the state capital. They emphasized that while the temporary special trains are an excellent immediate intervention, the long-term solution lies in introducing more daily express trains or increasing the coach composition of existing trains. Tourism and trade bodies in the city have also echoed these sentiments, stating that seamless connectivity is crucial for sustaining the economic momentum of the region.

In response to the positive feedback, senior railway officials at the Salem Division, under whose jurisdiction Coimbatore falls, stated that they are closely monitoring the occupancy rates of these special trains. They assured the public that if the patronage remains consistently high, they will actively recommend the extension of these services to the higher authorities in Chennai. The officials also highlighted that special maintenance protocols have been put in place at the Coimbatore Junction railway station to ensure the trains are cleaned, watered, and dispatched on time. This proactive stance by the Southern Railway has bolstered the confidence of local commuters, who hope for continued improvements in the district's rail infrastructure.

In a stern warning against bureaucratic apathy and administrative negligence, the Coimbatore Integrated Court Complex witnessed unprecedented scenes on Saturday, July 6, when the Fifth Additional District Court slapped a combined fine of Rs 62,000 on Coimbatore District Collector Kranthi Kumar Pati. The punitive action was taken by Judge Sivakumar after government prosecutors failed to appear for hearings in eight different cases scheduled for the day. This chronic absenteeism on the part of the prosecution brought the judicial proceedings to a grinding halt, causing immense frustration and waste of public resources. Legal experts in the city have noted that such a strong judicial reprimand against the district's highest administrative official is rare and reflects the court's growing impatience with systemic delays.

The first major lapse occurred during the hearing of a decade-old murder case originating from Karumathampatti, which has been dragging on in the Fifth Additional District Court for the last ten years. On Saturday, the accused was duly produced in court, and a medical doctor had traveled all the way from neighbouring Kerala to tender crucial witness testimony. However, because the Additional Public Prosecutor failed to show up, the court was unable to conduct the examination of the witness, forcing an unnecessary adjournment. Expressing strong displeasure at the state's failure, Judge Sivakumar noted that the delay was entirely due to the negligence of the prosecution and ordered the Coimbatore District Collector to pay a fine of Rs 10,000 within one week, directing that this amount be compensated to the doctor from Kerala to cover his travel expenses.

In another scheduled trial investigated by the Race Course Police, a similar situation unfolded, highlighting a pattern of systemic failure. An Assistant Sub-Inspector of Police had traveled a long distance from Tiruvarur to give evidence, and all five accused individuals in the case were also present in the courtroom. Once again, the prosecution's absence stalled the trial, prompting Judge Sivakumar to observe that both the witnesses and the accused were being subjected to unwarranted harassment and mental agony due to the state's lapse. Consequently, the court ordered the state government, represented by the Coimbatore District Collector, to deposit a fine of Rs 9,000 within a week. Out of this fine, Rs 4,000 is to be given to the police officer from Tiruvarur as travel compensation, while the five accused individuals are to receive Rs 1,000 each for their wasted time and resources.

This judicial crackdown has sent shockwaves through the local administration, directly impacting Coimbatore residents who are already burdened by a backlog of unresolved legal disputes. Local civic activists and litigants have welcomed the court’s decisive action, pointing out that ordinary citizens often lose their livelihoods and savings while attending endless court hearings only to have them postponed due to official absenteeism. The residents of Coimbatore, particularly those awaiting justice in long-pending criminal trials, hope that this heavy penalty of Rs 62,000 across eight cases will serve as a wake-up call for the district administration to streamline its legal department. The visible frustration of the public was echoed by lawyers at the Coimbatore bar, who stated that such negligence undermines the credibility of the entire criminal justice system.

Following the court's stringent order, senior officials at the Coimbatore District Collectorate have reportedly scrambled to address the embarrassing situation. While the District Collector's office has not released an official press statement yet, administrative sources indicate that an internal inquiry has been initiated to identify the specific prosecutors responsible for the lapses on Saturday. Senior officials are expected to coordinate with the public prosecution department to ensure that such embarrassing absences do not recur in the future, as the state must now arrange for the immediate disbursement of the fine amounts within the court-mandated one-week deadline. The legal fraternity in Coimbatore is closely watching how the district administration plans to reform its prosecution wing to prevent further delays in the delivery of justice.

The fine serves as a direct warning to the bureaucracy regarding their accountability to judicial proceedings.

The case stems from a long-pending dispute within Coimbatore district that required crucial inputs, counter-affidavits, and representation from the district administration. In a significant boost to the social and civic infrastructure of Coimbatore, the Rotary Club has announced a comprehensive suite of new community service projects aimed at addressing key developmental needs across the district. This major welfare initiative, designed to uplift underprivileged sections of society and enhance public amenities, was officially unveiled during a high-profile regional meeting of the organisation's leadership. The newly launched projects will primarily focus on critical areas such as healthcare accessibility, educational support for government school students, environmental conservation, and clean drinking water facilities. By targeting these essential sectors, the organisation aims to bridge the gaps in existing public infrastructure and foster sustainable development throughout the industrial hub of Coimbatore.

The decision to launch these targeted initiatives stems from a detailed assessment of local community needs, particularly in the suburban and rural pockets of the district where access to quality healthcare and modern educational resources remains limited. To ensure the maximum impact of these welfare schemes, the Rotary Club has mobilised substantial financial resources and partnered with local administrative bodies, educational institutions, and healthcare providers. Among the flagship projects is the establishment of advanced medical equipment clinics and the upgrading of infrastructure in several government-run schools. Additionally, the club plans to install high-capacity water purification plants in neglected residential neighborhoods to ensure that clean, potable water is readily available to thousands of families, thereby reducing the incidence of waterborne diseases in the region.

The official launch event was attended by prominent civic leaders, district administrators, and senior Rotary officials, who expressed their strong commitment to the welfare of Coimbatore's residents. The project coordinators also detailed the implementation timeline, assuring the public that the execution of these initiatives would be closely monitored to ensure transparency and timely completion.

Local residents and community activists have reacted with immense optimism to the announcement, noting that these interventions will directly improve their daily lives and ease financial burdens. Families living in the outer areas of the city, who often have to travel long distances for basic medical check-ups, are particularly enthusiastic about the proposed mobile healthcare clinics and diagnostic camps. By focusing on root-level issues, the newly introduced welfare model is set to create a healthier, more educated, and resilient populace in the region.

As the implementation phase begins, the Rotary Club has established dedicated committees comprising local volunteers and technical experts to oversee the day-to-day progress of each project. District authorities have assured full administrative cooperation, including the granting of necessary permissions and logistical support, to facilitate the smooth rollout of the infrastructure work. This collaborative effort is expected to set a new benchmark for corporate social responsibility and civic engagement in Coimbatore, inspiring other organisations to invest in the district's social capital. With a clear roadmap and strong community backing, these new service projects are poised to drive meaningful progress and enhance the overall quality of life for the people of Coimbatore in the coming years.

The pristine and highly popular eco-tourism destination of Kovai Kutralam, nestled in the foot slopes of the Western Ghats near Siruvani in Coimbatore district, has been temporarily closed to the public and tourists. The decision was taken by the Coimbatore Forest Division following a sudden and heavy spell of rainfall in the catchment areas of the Western Ghats, which led to a significant and rapid rise in the water flow at the Kovai Kutralam falls. Forest officials noted that the safety of visitors is of paramount importance, and allowing tourists near the cascading waters under such volatile weather conditions could lead to hazardous situations.

According to local forest rangers and administrative officials, the region has been experiencing intermittent heavy downpours over the last forty-eight hours, causing the mountain streams that feed the main waterfall to swell rapidly. Fearing flash floods and slippery rocky terrains, the forest department immediately deployed staff to clear the bathing areas and officially announced the suspension of entry for tourists until further notice. This preemptive measure is a standard safety protocol implemented by the Coimbatore administration whenever the water levels cross the danger threshold, ensuring that no untoward incidents occur in the dense forest zone.

The sudden closure has had a direct impact on both local residents and travellers from neighbouring districts who flock to the scenic spot, especially during the weekends, to escape the city heat. Several disappointed tourists who had travelled long distances to visit the falls had to be turned back at the Sadivayal check post, which serves as the entry point to the eco-tourism site. Local vendors, small tea stall owners, and transport operators in the Alandurai and Sadivayal areas have also expressed concern over the temporary shutdown, as their daily livelihoods are heavily dependent on the steady influx of visitors to the waterfalls.

In response to the situation, senior officials from the Coimbatore Forest Division have issued a public advisory urging people to cooperate with the department and avoid planning trips to the forest area for the next few days. Forest guards have been stationed at key vantage points and entry gates to monitor the water levels continuously and to prevent any unauthorized entry into the reserve forest. Official sources indicated that a thorough assessment of the weather conditions and water flow will be conducted daily, and the waterfalls will be reopened to the public only after the water flow subsides to a completely safe level.

Residents of Coimbatore have largely welcomed the timely intervention by the authorities, acknowledging that flash floods in these forest streams can be highly unpredictable and dangerous. The district administration has also requested the public to stay updated through official government press releases rather than relying on unverified social media reports regarding the status of the tourist spot. With the monsoon patterns showing increased activity across the Western Ghats, the Coimbatore Forest Department remains on high alert, prioritizing ecological preservation and public safety above all else.

In a major embarrassment for the Coimbatore district administration, the Madras High Court has imposed a total fine of Rs 62,000 on the Coimbatore District Collector in connection with eight different cases. The stringent action was taken by the court after the government advocate representing the district administration failed to appear for the scheduled hearings. This repeated absence of legal representation on behalf of the state authorities drew the ire of the high court judge, who viewed it as a serious lapse in administrative diligence. The fines were levied across these eight separate cases to emphasize the court's intolerance toward procedural delays and negligence in public litigation.

The root cause of this judicial reprimand lies in the persistent failure of the government counsel to present the administration's side during crucial court proceedings. In various cases involving land, local administration, and public welfare disputes in Coimbatore, the state's legal representatives were consistently absent when the matters were called for hearing. This lack of representation not only stalled the judicial process but also forced the high court to take a strict stance against the district's top bureaucrat. By imposing these monetary penalties directly on the District Collector's office, the court has sent a clear message regarding the accountability of public officials and their legal teams in handling government litigation.

This administrative failure has a direct and distressing impact on the local residents of Coimbatore, many of whom are litigants in these long-pending cases. Common citizens often spend years navigating the legal system, investing their hard-earned money and time, only to face endless delays because government officials fail to show up in court. When state representatives do not appear, hearings are routinely adjourned, which indefinitely postpones justice for ordinary residents seeking resolution on critical civic and personal grievances. The local population has expressed deep frustration over this systemic negligence, noting that such lapses undermine public trust in both the district administration and the judicial process.

In the wake of this penal action, the official response from the Coimbatore district administration has been one of damage control and internal scrutiny. Sources close to the Collectorate indicate that an inquiry has been initiated to identify the specific lapses that led to the non-appearance of the government advocate in these eight cases. Senior officials are reportedly reviewing the communication channels between the Collector’s office and the state legal team to ensure that court summons and hearing dates are tracked more efficiently in the future. There is also pressure from administrative quarters to hold the concerned legal officers accountable for this significant lapse, which has brought unwanted negative publicity to the district's leadership.

Moving forward, this incident is expected to prompt urgent reforms in how the Coimbatore district administration manages its legal affairs and coordinates with government pleaders. Legal experts and civic activists in the city have called for a dedicated monitoring cell within the Collectorate to oversee all active court cases and guarantee timely representation. For the residents of Coimbatore, the hope remains that this strict judicial intervention will serve as a wake-up call for bureaucrats, leading to a more responsive, responsible, and transparent administrative machinery. Until such structural changes are implemented, the shadow of this judicial penalty will continue to loom over the local governance of the region.
