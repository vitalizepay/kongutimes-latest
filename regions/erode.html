The agitation, which has been gainfully gathering momentum for over a month, saw hundreds of farmers assembling at key junctions to voice their grievances against the current credit policies. The primary catalyst for this widespread agitation is the compounding financial crisis faced by the agrarian community due to erratic monsoon patterns and rising input costs. High rates of interest, coupled with the rising costs of seeds, fertilisers, and diesel, have pushed many small and marginal landholders into a vicious cycle of debt. According to local agricultural union leaders, the existing partial relief measures have proven entirely inadequate, making a comprehensive, unconditional loan waiver the only viable solution to prevent widespread rural bankruptcy.

Faced with the growing intensity of the demonstrations, district administration officials and representatives from the cooperative department have stepped in to defuse the situation. Despite these official assurances, the farmers of Erode remain steadfast in their resolve, stating that they have heard similar promises in the past with little to no field-level implementation. Local agricultural associations have declared that their sit-ins and peaceful demonstrations will continue in a phased manner across the district until a formal government order is officially gazetted. As the stand-off enters its second month, the local community remains on edge, hoping for a speedy resolution that can restore normalcy to the fields and markets of this vital agricultural belt.

Commuters traveling between Erode and Salem were caught off guard on Friday morning as private bus operators in the region abruptly hiked ticket fares without any prior official announcement. The sudden fare revision, which saw ticket prices rise by ten to twenty rupees depending on the distance, led to heated arguments between passengers and bus conductors at the central bus stands in both Erode and Salem. Daily wage laborers, college students, and regular office-goers who rely heavily on these private services for their daily commute expressed deep frustration over the unexpected financial burden. Many passengers claimed they were forced to shell out the extra amount as they had no other viable transport alternatives to reach their destinations on time during the morning rush hour.

According to representatives from the private bus operators' association in Erode, the decision to increase the fares was necessitated by the continuous rise in operational costs, including skyrocketing prices of diesel, spare parts, and maintenance charges. The operators argued that they had been running services at a loss for several months and could no longer sustain operations under the previous tariff structure. They claimed that while government buses receive subsidies and financial support from the state, private operators must bear the entire brunt of inflation and rising toll gate charges on the national highways. Despite these explanations, commuters remained highly unsatisfied, pointing out that unilateral fare hikes without government approval are entirely illegal and exploitative.

The impact of this sudden price hike has been severely felt across the local community in Erode, particularly among those from lower-income households who travel daily for work. Several commuters noted that a round-trip journey that previously cost them relatively less now consumes a significant portion of their daily earnings. Local passenger welfare associations have strongly condemned the move, stating that private operators are taking advantage of the high demand on the busy Erode-Salem route. Activists have pointed out that the timing of the hike is particularly harsh as families are already struggling with the rising costs of essential commodities and educational expenses for the new academic year.

In response to the growing public outrage and subsequent protests at the Erode bus stand, officials from the Regional Transport Office (RTO) initiated an immediate inquiry into the matter. Senior transport department officials stated that no official permission or government order had been issued to sanction any hike in bus fares for private operators in the district. The RTO has deployed special inspection teams at key transit points, including the Erode central bus stand and the Chithode bypass, to monitor the situation and verify the ticket prices being charged by private operators. Authorities have warned that strict legal action, including the temporary suspension of permits and heavy financial penalties, will be imposed on any private bus found violating the government-approved fare structure.

As the standoff between passengers and bus operators continues, local residents are urging the state government to increase the frequency of government-run Tamil Nadu State Transport Corporation (TNSTC) buses on the Erode-Salem route. Commuters believe that introducing more government services would break the monopoly of private operators and provide a reliable, low-cost alternative for the public. For now, transport officials have assured the public that normalcy will be restored quickly and that operators have been strictly instructed to revert to the original fares immediately. The district administration is closely monitoring the development to ensure that commuters are not subjected to further harassment or illegal overcharging.

இப்பிரச்சினை குறித்து தகவல் அறிந்தவுடன், ஈரோடு மின் பகிர்மான வட்டத்தின் உதவி செயற்பொறியாளர் தலைமையிலான மின்வாரிய ஊழியர்கள் மற்றும் தொழில்நுட்பக் குழுவினர் உடனடியாக சம்பவ இடத்திற்கு விரைந்து வந்தனர். நவீன பழுது கண்டறியும் கருவிகளைக் கொண்டு, மேட்டூர் சாலையின் குறிப்பிட்ட பகுதியில் தோண்டி, பழுதடைந்த மின் கேபிளை சீரமைக்கும் பணியில் தீவிரமாக ஈடுபட்டனர். இரவு முழுதும் நீடித்த இந்த சீரமைப்புப் பணியில், கூடுதல் பணியாளர்கள் ஈடுபடுத்தப்பட்டு பழுதடைந்த பகுதிகள் அகற்றப்பட்டு புதிய கேபிள்கள் இணைக்கப்பட்டன. மின்வாரியப் பணியாளர்களின் இந்த போர்க்கால நடவடிக்கையால், பல மணி நேரப் போராட்டத்திற்குப் பிறகு மின் விநியோகம் படிப்படியாக சீரமைக்கப்பட்டது.

மின்சார வாரிய அதிகாரிகள் தரப்பில் இது குறித்துக் கூறுகையில், ஈரோடு மாநகரப் பகுதியில் தடையற்ற மின்சாரம் வழங்குவதற்காகவே புதைவட மின் கேபிள்கள் அமைக்கப்பட்டுள்ளதாக விளக்கமளித்தனர். எனினும், எதிர்பாராத நிலத்தடி நீர் கசிவு மற்றும் இதர பணிகளால் ஏற்படும் அதிர்வுகள் காரணமாக இதுபோன்ற கோளாறுகள் எப்போதாவது ஏற்படுவது தவிர்க்க முடியாததாகிறது என்றும் கூறினர். வருங்காலங்களில் இதுபோன்ற மின்தடை அசம்பாவிதங்கள் மீண்டும் ஏற்படாமல் தடுக்க, அதிநவீன தொழில்நுட்பக் கருவிகள் மூலம் தொடர்ந்து கண்காணிப்புப் பணிகள் மேற்கொள்ளப்படும் என்று உறுதியளித்தனர். மேலும், பொதுமக்கள் இதுபோன்ற அவசர காலங்களில் மின்வாரியத்திற்கு உடனடியாகத் தகவல் வழங்கி ஒத்துழைப்பு நல்க வேண்டும் என்றும் அதிகாரிகள் கேட்டுக் கொண்டனர்.

Power outage in Erode Mettur Road due to underground electricity cable damage

A major technical snag in the underground power cable network plunged several key areas along Erode’s bustling Mettur Road into complete darkness on Saturday night, causing severe hardship to local residents and commercial establishments. The power outage, which began unexpectedly in the late evening, disrupted the daily routine of hundreds of families and brought business activities to a grinding halt in one of the city's most prominent commercial zones. Shopkeepers and small business owners were forced to halt their operations prematurely, while residents in the surrounding residential pockets had to endure a long, stifling night without fans, air conditioning, or domestic water supply. The sudden blackout also raised safety concerns among pedestrians and motorists navigating the busy thoroughfare, which was illuminated only by the headlights of passing vehicles.

According to sources within the Tamil Nadu Generation and Distribution Corporation (TANGEDCO), the disruption was caused by an unexpected technical fault in the high-tension underground electricity cables running beneath Mettur Road. These underground cables, which were laid to ensure uninterrupted power supply and prevent accidents associated with overhead lines, suffered a severe internal rupture that triggered an automatic shutdown of the local feeder line. Locating the exact point of the damage proved to be an arduous task for the field staff, as the fault was buried deep beneath the concrete road surface, requiring specialized diagnostic equipment to pinpoint the rupture. This delayed the commencement of the physical repair work, prolonging the duration of the power outage well into the early hours of the morning.

The impact of the prolonged blackout was felt acutely by the local community, particularly the elderly, children, and hospital patients in the vicinity who had to struggle through the humid night. Many residents took to the streets in frustration, expressing their anger over the delay in restoring power and the lack of immediate communication from the electricity board. Commercial establishments, including restaurants, textile showrooms, and grocery stores along Mettur Road, reported significant financial losses as they had to rely on expensive diesel generators to sustain basic lighting, while those without backup power were forced to close their shops early on a peak business night. Local youth and community volunteers assisted elderly neighbors and helped guide traffic at major intersections where the streetlights and traffic signals had gone completely dark.

Upon receiving numerous complaints from the affected public and local traders' associations, senior TANGEDCO officials immediately dispatched an emergency maintenance crew to the site to rectify the issue on a war footing. A dedicated team of line inspectors and technical experts arrived with excavation machinery and cable-jointing kits to begin the restoration process under the supervision of assistant engineers. The repair workers had to carefully dig up a portion of the busy roadside pavement to access the damaged underground cable without disrupting other utility pipelines, such as drinking water mains and telecom lines. Despite the challenging night-time working conditions and the complexity of repairing high-voltage underground infrastructure, the technical team worked tirelessly to splice the ruptured cables and reinforce the insulation.

Power supply was eventually restored to the affected areas of Mettur Road in a phased manner after several hours of intense repair work, bringing much-needed relief to the weary citizens of Erode. TANGEDCO officials later stated that the damage was likely caused by wear and tear compounded by recent soil moisture fluctuations, and they assured the public that preventive maintenance checks would be intensified across the city's underground cable network to avert such incidents in the future. Local civic activists, while appreciating the hard work of the ground staff, have urged the municipal corporation and the electricity board to establish a more robust rapid-response mechanism and install automated fault-detection systems to minimize the downtime during such utility emergencies.
