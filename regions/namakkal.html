இந்த அவல நிலைக்கு நகராட்சி நிர்வாகத்தின் அலட்சியப் போக்கும், போதிய முன்னெச்சரிக்கை நடவடிக்கைகளை எடுக்காததுமே முக்கியக் காரணம் என்று பொதுமக்கள் குற்றம் சாட்டுகின்றனர். மழைக்காலம் தொடங்குவதற்கு முன்னரே வடிகால்களைத் தூர்வாரி, அடைப்புகளைச் சீரமைக்க வேண்டும் என்று பலமுறை கோரிக்கை விடுத்தும் அதிகாரிகள் எந்தவித நடவடிக்கையும் எடுக்கவில்லை எனத் தெரிகிறது. இதனால் தற்போது பெய்த மிதமான மழையிலேயே நகரின் முக்கிய வடிகால்கள் அனைத்தும் நிரம்பி வழிந்து, தண்ணீர் குடியிருப்புப் பகுதிகளுக்குள் புகுந்துவிட்டது. குறிப்பாக, கழிவுநீர் கால்வாய்கள் முறையாகப் பராமரிக்கப்படாததால், மழைநீருடன் கழிவுநீரும் கலந்து தெருக்களில் ஆறாக ஓடுவதுடன் பெரும் துர்நாற்றத்தையும் ஏற்படுத்தி வருகிறது.

நாமக்கல் நகராட்சியின் 12-வது வார்டுக்கு உட்பட்ட கணேசபுரம், நல்லிபாளையம் மற்றும் அதன் சுற்றுவட்டாரப் பகுதிகளில் தான் இந்த பாதிப்பு மிகக் கடுமையாகக் காணப்படுகிறது. இப்பகுதிகளில் வசிக்கும் முதியவர்கள், குழந்தைகள் மற்றும் கர்ப்பிணிப் பெண்கள் வீட்டை விட்டு வெளியே வர முடியாமல் தவித்து வருகின்றனர். மேலும், தெருக்களில் தேங்கியுள்ள தேக்க நீரினால் கொசுக்கள் உற்பத்தி அதிகரித்து, டெங்கு மற்றும் மலேரியா போன்ற தொற்றுநோய்கள் பரவும் அபாயம் ஏற்பட்டுள்ளதாக அஞ்சப்படுகிறது. அப்பகுதி மக்கள் தங்களின் அத்தியாவசியப் பொருட்களான பால், குடிநீர் மற்றும் காய்கறிகளை வாங்குவதற்குக் கூட முழங்கால் அளவு தண்ணீரில் நடந்து செல்ல வேண்டிய அவல நிலைக்குத் தள்ளப்பட்டுள்ளனர்.

Residents and motorists navigating the busy arterial roads of Namakkal have raised serious concerns over the rapidly deteriorating condition of local transport infrastructure, urging municipal authorities to take immediate corrective action. Several key stretches across the town, which serve as vital transit routes for both heavy commercial vehicles and daily commuters, have developed deep potholes and uneven patches following recent spells of heavy rainfall. The poor state of these roads has not only severely disrupted the smooth flow of vehicular traffic but has also significantly increased the risk of road accidents, particularly for two-wheeler riders during the night. Local trade merchants and shopkeepers operating along these damaged routes complain that the constant dust kicked up by passing vehicles is affecting their businesses and causing respiratory discomfort to pedestrians.

The primary cause of this widespread damage has been attributed to a combination of inadequate stormwater drainage systems and the high volume of heavy cargo trucks that frequently traverse Namakkal, a major national hub for the poultry and transport industries. When heavy rains lash the region, water accumulates on the road surfaces instead of draining away, weakening the asphalt base and leading to rapid erosion under the weight of multi-axle trucks. Commuters point out that while patch-up works are occasionally carried out by contractors, these temporary measures fail to survive even a single season of monsoon showers. Frustrated residents have highlighted that key intersections near the government hospital and the main bus stand have become particularly hazardous, requiring motorists to navigate carefully to avoid collisions.

In response to the growing public outcry, representatives from local civic welfare associations have formally submitted a memorandum to the Namakkal municipality and the state highways department demanding a permanent solution to the crisis. Community activists emphasize that the current state of the roads is unacceptable for a city that generates substantial revenue for the state government through its thriving transport sector. They argue that the lack of periodic maintenance and high-quality relaying of roads has resulted in a recurring waste of public funds on ineffective repairs. School authorities have also expressed anxiety over the safety of buses and vans ferrying young children through these pothole-riddled stretches during peak morning hours.

Acknowledging the severity of the situation, senior officials from the Namakkal municipal administration and the highways department have assured the public that a comprehensive restoration plan is being drafted to address the issue systematically. According to official sources, a detailed survey of the worst-affected stretches has already been initiated to estimate the budget required for laying high-durability concrete roads in high-traffic zones. The department plans to prioritize the repair of main arterial roads before expanding the scope of work to cover interior residential streets. Furthermore, officials have promised to inspect the existing roadside drains and clear blockages to ensure that rainwater does not stagnate on the newly laid surfaces in the future.

While the assurances from the local administration have provided some hope to the aggrieved residents, the community remains vigilant and is demanding a strict timeline for the commencement and completion of the roadworks. Local residents’ welfare groups have stated that they will continue to monitor the progress of the promised road restoration project and hold the concerned departments accountable if delays persist. As Namakkal continues to grow as a vital industrial and commercial node in western Tamil Nadu, the demand for robust, safe, and well-maintained road infrastructure remains a top priority for its citizens. For now, motorists are being advised to exercise extreme caution while navigating the damaged stretches, especially during poor visibility conditions.

The Namakkal Veterinary College and Research Institute (VCRI), a premier institution under the Tamil Nadu Veterinary and Animal Sciences University (TANUVAS), has officially inaugurated its newly constructed additional campus buildings to support its growing academic and research needs. The state-of-the-art facilities were formally opened in a high-profile ceremony aimed at bolstering the infrastructure of one of the region’s most vital educational hubs. Namakkal Member of Parliament (MP) V. S. Matheswaran graced the occasion as the chief guest, leading the ribbon-cutting ceremony alongside senior university administrators, faculty members, and local department heads. This major infrastructural expansion represents a significant step forward in meeting the rising demand for veterinary education and research capabilities in western Tamil Nadu.

The newly inaugurated blocks have been designed to accommodate the increasing student intake and to provide advanced laboratory spaces for cutting-edge veterinary research. The expansion includes spacious lecture halls equipped with modern audio-visual aids, sophisticated diagnostic laboratories, and administrative offices designed to streamline college operations. According to college officials, the project was conceptualised to address the pressing need for modernised spaces where students can engage in practical training and hands-on clinical research. The funding for this extensive construction was sanctioned through state educational development grants, reflecting the government's commitment to upgrading specialized agricultural and veterinary sciences infrastructure in the Kongu region.

For the residents and farming communities of Namakkal and its surrounding districts, this development holds immense practical value as the region is a major hub for poultry farming and animal husbandry. The upgrade of the veterinary college directly translates into better diagnostic services, superior veterinary healthcare, and quicker intervention strategies for livestock diseases that frequently affect local poultry and dairy farmers. Local livestock owners will now have access to advanced referral clinics and specialized testing facilities, which will help safeguard their livelihood from devastating animal epidemics. Furthermore, the presence of an upgraded research facility ensures that local farmers receive the latest scientific guidance and training workshops to improve their yield and veterinary practices.

During his address at the inaugural ceremony, MP V. S. Matheswaran emphasized the central role that Namakkal plays in the state's agricultural economy and praised the college for its continuous service to the farming community. He stated that the central and state governments are highly focused on empowering rural youth through quality technical education, and these new facilities would help produce world-class veterinary professionals. The MP also assured the college administration of continued support for future expansion projects, noting that robust veterinary infrastructure is essential for sustaining the district’s status as a leader in poultry production. Other officiating dignitaries, including the college dean and senior faculty, expressed their gratitude and highlighted how the new space would immediately benefit ongoing postgraduate research projects.

The successful completion and inauguration of these additional buildings mark a new chapter in the academic journey of the Namakkal Veterinary College and Research Institute. Students and research scholars have expressed great enthusiasm about the new facilities, noting that the advanced labs will significantly enhance their practical learning experience and research outcomes. With the new academic year underway, the college administration plans to fully utilize the new blocks immediately, ensuring that both undergraduate and postgraduate students benefit from the upgraded environment. This infrastructural milestone not only elevates the academic status of the institution but also reinforces Namakkal's position as a pioneering district in veterinary excellence and rural development.

The bustling Namakkal Uzhavar Sandhai (farmers' market) witnessed an extraordinary surge in commercial activity on Saturday, recording a massive sale of 44 tonnes of fresh vegetables and fruits. Situated in the heart of Namakkal town, this state-run agricultural market serves as a vital bridge between local cultivators and urban consumers. Driven by the weekend demand and an abundant supply of fresh seasonal produce from the surrounding rural pockets of the district, thousands of local residents flocked to the market right from the early hours of the morning. Officials from the agricultural marketing department noted that the day's transaction value scaled new heights, reflecting a highly successful trading day for the regional farming community.

According to market administrative sources, a total of 184 farmers from various villages across the Namakkal district brought their freshly harvested produce directly to the stalls. The market recorded an impressive footfall of over 6,550 consumers who prioritized purchasing directly from the growers to ensure quality and freshness. The total transaction value for the single day's sales was officially pegged at approximately Rs. 18.52 lakhs, highlighting the robust economic cycle within the local agricultural sector. This direct-to-consumer model successfully eliminated intermediaries, allowing farmers to secure competitive prices for their hard work while ensuring that urban buyers did not have to pay inflated retail prices.

The local residents of Namakkal expressed great satisfaction with the overall management of the Uzhavar Sandhai, highlighting the affordability and superior quality of the goods compared to private retail outlets. Regular shoppers pointed out that vegetables like tomatoes, onions, drumsticks, and various green leafy vegetables were available in abundance and at rates regulated strictly by the government committee. This price stability has provided immense relief to middle-class and lower-income families who are navigating general inflationary pressures in the broader retail market. Furthermore, the hygienic environment and organized stall arrangements have made the weekly shopping experience highly convenient for the citizens of the town.

To handle the massive weekend rush, the market officials and administrative staff had put in place elaborate arrangements to facilitate smooth transactions and hassle-free movement. The authorities ensured that price list boards were prominently displayed at the entrance, updated with the day's government-approved rates to maintain absolute transparency and prevent any overcharging. Additional security personnel and cleaning staff were deployed across the premises to manage the crowd effectively and maintain sanitation standards throughout the peak operating hours. The administration also monitored the weighing scales used by the farmers to guarantee fair trade practices and build consumer trust.

Looking ahead, the Namakkal district agricultural marketing department officials have assured continued support to both farmers and the public to sustain this positive momentum. Plans are reportedly underway to further upgrade the basic infrastructure at the Uzhavar Sandhai, including cold storage facilities and better waste management systems, to minimize post-harvest losses. Officials have also appealed to more local farmers from the remote corners of the district to utilize this government platform to sell their produce directly, promising them all necessary logistical assistance. This successful weekend sale stands as a testament to the crucial role that the Uzhavar Sandhai plays in strengthening the rural economy of Namakkal.

In a surprise move that has brought immense relief to the local commuters of Erumapatti, the Namakkal regional transport department officials conducted a rigorous late-night inspection targeting both government and private buses. Transport officials closely monitored the routes of all scheduled services operating between Namakkal, Trichy, and nearby destinations. During the inspection, several state-run Tamil Nadu State Transport Corporation (TNSTC) buses and private stage carriages were caught attempting to skip the Erumapatti town limits by taking bypass roads. The enforcement team intercepted these vehicles on the spot, forcing the drivers to divert back into the town terminal to safely discharge their passengers as originally scheduled.

According to local residents, this decisive action by the authorities was long overdue as the skipping of bus stops had become a daily nightmare. Commuters have been urged to note down the registration numbers and timings of any buses that fail to enter the designated bus stands and report them directly to the regional transport office. This proactive stance by the administration has sent a strong warning to transport operators, restoring public confidence in the local public transit system.

The picturesque slopes of the Kolli Hills in Namakkal district witnessed a massive influx of domestic tourists over the weekend, as families and nature enthusiasts gathered to escape the seasonal heat. Following recent spells of moderate to heavy rainfall in the Eastern Ghats catchment areas, the region's famous waterfalls, including the Masila Falls and Agaya Gangai, have regained their majestic flow. Visitors from neighbouring districts such as Salem, Karur, Tiruchirappalli, and Erode queued up early in the morning to access the scenic spots. The rejuvenated water cascades provided a perfect setting for travellers who were seen enthusiastically bathing and spending quality time amidst the lush green surroundings. Local merchants reported a significant surge in footfall, marking a vibrant start to the weekend tourism cycle in the hill station.

This sudden surge in tourism has been largely attributed to the pleasant weather conditions prevailing across the Kolli Hills region after a brief period of dry spells. Additionally, the district forest department's recent initiatives to clean up the pathways leading to the waterfalls have encouraged families with children to visit the spot. Travel operators in Namakkal noted that the combination of school reopening anxieties easing and the refreshing climate made Kolli Hills the preferred destination for a quick weekend getaway. The availability of fresh mountain produce, such as jackfruit and spices, further added to the charm of the tourist experience.

While the influx of visitors has brought joy to the hospitality sector, it has also created mixed feelings among the local tribal residents of the hills. On one hand, small-scale roadside vendors, homestay owners, and jeep drivers have welcomed the increased economic activity, which provides them with a vital source of livelihood. On the other hand, residents in villages like Solakkadu and Semmedu have raised concerns regarding traffic congestion on the narrow ghat roads and the irresponsible disposal of plastic waste by some tourists. Community leaders have urged visitors to respect the fragile ecology of the hill station and avoid littering near the pristine water sources. The local panchayat has deployed temporary sanitation workers to collect garbage, but residents feel a more permanent waste management system is needed to handle the growing weekend crowds.

In response to the heavy rush, the Namakkal district administration and the local police department have implemented several safety and crowd control measures. Additional police personnel have been stationed at key intersections and near the waterfalls to prevent any untoward incidents or accidents in the slippery rocky terrains. Forest department officials are actively monitoring the entry points to ensure that visitors do not carry banned plastic items or alcohol into the reserve forest areas. Warning signs have also been erected at deeper sections of the waterfalls to caution swimmers about the strong undercurrents. The district authorities have appealed to the public to cooperate with the security personnel on duty and strictly adhere to the designated bathing timings for their own safety.

Looking ahead, the district administration plans to introduce more regulated tourism initiatives to balance ecological conservation with economic development in the Kolli Hills. Discussions are reportedly underway to establish dedicated parking zones near Semmedu to ease the traffic bottlenecks on the main routes. Local youth are also being trained as eco-guides to help tourists navigate the trekking trails safely while educating them about the unique biodiversity of the Namakkal region. Officials believe that with proper infrastructure upgrades and community participation, Kolli Hills can be developed into a premier sustainable eco-tourism destination in Tamil Nadu. For now, the local administration remains on high alert to manage the ongoing seasonal rush and ensure a hassle-free experience for all visitors.

இந்த திடீர் விலை உயர்வு நுகர்வோரான சாமானிய மக்கள் மத்தியில் பெரும் தாக்கத்தை ஏற்படுத்தியுள்ளது. வார இறுதி நாட்களான சனி மற்றும் ஞாயிற்றுக்கிழமைகளில் அசைவ உணவுகளை அதிகம் விரும்பும் நடுத்தரக் குடும்பங்கள், இந்த விலை உயர்வால் தங்கள் மாதாந்திர பட்ஜெட்டில் கூடுதல் சுமையைச் சந்திக்க நேரிடும் என்று கவலை தெரிவிக்கின்றனர். சில்லறை விற்பனை நிலையங்களில் கறிக்கோழி இறைச்சியின் விலை கிலோவுக்கு 10 முதல் 15 ரூபாய் வரை அதிகரிக்க வாய்ப்புள்ளதால், ஹோட்டல் உரிமையாளர்களும் தங்களின் உணவுப் பொருட்களின் விலையை உயர்த்த வேண்டிய கட்டாயத்திற்குத் தள்ளப்பட்டுள்ளனர். இருப்பினும், கடந்த சில வாரங்களாக நஷ்டத்தைச் சந்தித்து வந்த கோழிப் பண்ணையாளர்களுக்கு இந்த 2 ரூபாய் உயர்வு ஓரளவிற்கு நிம்மதியைத் தந்துள்ளதாகத் தொழில்துறையினர் குறிப்பிடுகின்றனர்.

The poultry industry in the Namakkal region of Tamil Nadu has witnessed a notable shift as the wholesale price of broiler chicken was increased by Rs 2 per kilogram. This decision was finalized during a key meeting of the National Egg Coordination Committee (NECC) and the Broiler Coordination Committee (BCC) held at their regional headquarters in Namakkal. Following the review of market dynamics, the price of live broiler chicken has been revised upward from its previous rate of Rs 118 per kilogram to Rs 120 per kilogram. Industry experts attribute this marginal hike to a combination of rising production costs, increased feed prices, and a steady growth in demand across the neighboring districts and states.

Broiler farming in Namakkal serves as a major economic backbone for the Western zone of Tamil Nadu, supplying poultry products to Kerala, Karnataka, and various domestic markets. Representatives from the poultry farming federation explained that the cost of essential poultry feed ingredients, such as maize and soya, has risen significantly over the last few quarter periods, forcing them to adjust the wholesale rates to sustain their operational margins. Additionally, the recent fluctuations in seasonal temperatures had slightly impacted the bird weight gain cycles, leading to a tighter supply chain. Officials from the Broiler Coordination Committee stated that this minor price correction was absolutely necessary to prevent local poultry farmers from incurring heavy financial losses.

The price hike has triggered immediate reactions among the local residents and retail consumers across Namakkal district, who are already grappling with the rising costs of essential commodities. Local meat vendors and hoteliers in the region have indicated that they might have to pass this additional cost onto the end consumers if the wholesale prices continue to climb in the coming weeks. For middle-class families in Namakkal, chicken has long been an affordable source of protein compared to mutton or seafood, and any upward revision in its price directly affects their weekly household budgets. Retailers in key market areas like Paramathi Velur, Rasipuram, and Tiruchengode have reported a cautious approach from buyers who are closely monitoring the fluctuating meat prices.

In response to the concerns raised by consumer forums regarding the sudden price revision, officials from the regional animal husbandry department and poultry associations have urged the public not to panic. They emphasized that the current price of Rs 120 per kilogram is a wholesale benchmark and that retail prices would stabilize once the supply-demand gap is bridged in the market. Industry spokespersons also reassured the public that there is no shortage of broiler stock in the Namakkal zone, which houses hundreds of high-capacity poultry farms. The authorities are actively monitoring the distribution channels to ensure that retail traders do not engage in artificial hoarding or exploit the wholesale price hike to overcharge local consumers.

Looking ahead, market analysts in Namakkal predict that the poultry prices may experience further minor adjustments depending on the upcoming festival season and the monsoon patterns across South India. Since Namakkal is a premier hub for both egg and poultry production in the country, the decisions made by the local committee have a cascading effect on the entire southern poultry market. Farmers are hoping for a reduction in feed import duties and state-level subsidies to help stabilize production costs in the long run. For now, the stakeholders in the Namakkal poultry sector remain optimistic that the current price hike will offer temporary relief to the struggling farmers while keeping the market viable for traders and consumers alike.
